# NYC Yellow Taxi — January 2019: Anomaly Report
**Sphere:** `nyc_taxi_2019_jan`
**Run:** 0.1.0 / Experiment A / Round 1
**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Tool:** hypertopos (GDS navigator)

---

## Executive Summary

Seven distinct anomaly classes were identified across 7,556,807 trips and 265 pickup/dropoff
zones. The most severe involve a handful of trips with technically impossible or nonsensical
values — fare of $623,259, tip percentage of 4,500%, trip distance of 832 miles — alongside
structural data-quality defects: 114,783 trips recording zero passengers, 76,165 trips from
an undocumented Vendor ID 4, 103 trips with the invalid rate code 99, and 370 records with
pickup timestamps from before or after January 2019. Several zone-level anomalies reflect
genuine operational realities (airports, isolated Staten Island routes) rather than errors.
One population-wide regime shift occurred on 2019-02-19 and is likely a reporting artifact
from late-arriving data.

## Finding 1 — Catastrophic Fare Outlier: $623,259.86

**Confidence: Very High**

**Evidence:**
- `fare_amount` max = **$623,259.86** vs p99 = $52.00, mean = $12.24, median = $9.00
- The ratio of max to p99 is **11,986×** — flagged as "extreme cluster" by sphere profiling
- Only **3 trips** have `fare_amount > $1,000`, all from **Vendor 1 (Creative Mobile Technologies)**
- 8 trips have `fare_amount > $500` spread across 7 pickup zones; the $623,259.86 value
  appears in zones 237 (Upper East Side South) and 90 (Elmhurst/Maspeth)
- The max fare for Vendor 2 (VeriFone) is capped at $613.00 — a plausible airport-via-Nassau
  negotiated fare — while Vendor 1 allows unbounded values
- Zone 237 (Upper East Side South) is the top anomalous zone in `zone_activity_pattern`
  (delta_norm = 10.63, severity = high) with `_d_max_fare` contributing 51.7% of its anomaly
  score. Repair requires zeroing out max_fare, total_fare, and trip_count simultaneously,
  confirming this is a single corrupted record pulling the whole zone profile.

**Interpretation:**
A data entry or meter error produced a single trip fare of $623,259.86. This is not a
legitimate charge. The value is ~50,000× the median fare and exceeds the annual revenue of
most taxi drivers. It contaminates zone-level fare statistics for Upper East Side South and
Elmhurst/Maspeth. The Vendor 1 system appears to lack an upper-bound validation on fare
values that the Vendor 2 system enforces.

## Finding 2 — Tip Percentage of 4,500%

**Confidence: Very High**

**Evidence:**
- `tip_pct` max = **4,500** (i.e. 450,000%) vs p99 = 0.389, mean = 0.157, median = 0.209
- The ratio max/p99 is **11,571×**
- **4,484 trips** have `tip_pct > 1.0` (i.e. tip exceeds the fare); almost all are credit-card
  payments (4,484 of 4,485 cases)
- **81 trips** have `tip_pct > 10` across all three vendors
- Only **1 cash trip** shows `tip_pct > 1.0` — cash tips are not recorded by TLC meters, so
  this record is doubly suspicious
- The extreme 4,500 value comes from Vendor 1; Vendor 2's max is 60.0, Vendor 4's max is 121.12

**Interpretation:**
The 4,500 tip_pct value is almost certainly a data entry error — likely a misplaced decimal
(e.g. $45.00 tip on a $1.00 fare). The broader tail of 4,484 trips with tip > fare deserves
scrutiny: many may reflect legitimate over-tipping or rounding artifacts, but the extreme end
represents clear input errors. Cash tip values of zero are expected (meters don't capture them),
making the single non-zero cash tip with tip_pct > 1 suspicious.

## Finding 3 — Impossible Trip Distance: 831.8 Miles

**Confidence: Very High**

**Evidence:**
- `trip_distance` max = **831.8 miles** vs p99 = 18.9 miles, mean = 2.83 miles, median = 1.55 miles
- Ratio max/p99 = **44×** — flagged as extreme cluster
- **26 trips** have `trip_distance > 100 miles` (17 from Vendor 2, 9 from Vendor 1)
- NYC to Los Angeles is ~2,800 miles; 831.8 miles would reach Chicago from NYC
- Speed is capped at 100 mph in the dataset; no trip has recorded speed > 100 mph, so these
  extreme distances are inconsistent with recorded trip durations (max 300 min = 5 hours;
  831 miles / 5 hours = 166 mph — physically impossible in Manhattan traffic)

**Interpretation:**
GPS odometer glitches or meter malfunctions produce sporadic extreme distance readings. These
26 trips (out of 7.5M, or 0.0003%) are individually identifiable data errors. They do not
reflect actual travel.

## Finding 4 — 114,783 Trips with Zero Passengers (Vendor 1 Concentrated)

**Confidence: High**

**Evidence:**
- `passenger_count` min = 0; **114,783 trips** record `passenger_count < 1` (i.e. exactly 0)
- Of these: **114,725 (99.95%)** are from Vendor 1 (Creative Mobile Technologies), only
  **58 from Vendor 2** (VeriFone)
- This is 3.95% of all Vendor 1 trips vs 0.001% of Vendor 2 trips — a 3,000× difference
- The Vendor 1 passenger distribution (mean 1.20, std 0.64) is tighter than Vendor 2
  (mean 1.81, std 1.44), which also records trips up to 9 passengers vs Vendor 1's max of 7

**Interpretation:**
Zero-passenger trips are most likely dispatch errors, meter-on-without-passenger events, or
driver testing. The extreme concentration in Vendor 1 points to a systematic difference in how
that vendor's dispatch system handles unoccupied or test trips — their software may allow
meter activation without a passenger confirmation step that Vendor 2 requires. This is a
data-quality issue that should be filtered before any demand or revenue analysis.

## Finding 5 — Undocumented Vendor ID 4 (76,165 Trips)

**Confidence: High**

**Evidence:**
- The sphere's `vendors` line contains 2 entries: ID 1 (Creative Mobile Technologies) and
  ID 2 (VeriFone Inc). TLC officially licenses only these two vendors for yellow taxi meters.
- **76,165 trips** carry `VendorID = 4` — not mapped to any registered vendor name
- Vendor 4 trips have: avg fare $11.58 (close to normal), avg speed 11.89 mph (normal),
  max fare $215 (plausible), max tip_pct 121 (suspicious but not extreme), passenger count
  range 1–5 (mean 1.02 — almost all solo)
- Vendor 4 has 81 trips with `tip_pct > 1.0` and 3 with `tip_pct > 10`
- The vendor aggregation using `group_by_line=vendors` only returns 2 rows (IDs 1 and 2),
  confirming ID 4 is not joined to any registered entity

**Interpretation:**
Vendor ID 4 is either: (a) a data encoding error where some Vendor 1 or 2 records were
mislabeled, (b) a third-party dispatch app that shares yellow taxi infrastructure, or (c) a
test/staging system that leaked into production data. The volume (76K trips) is too large to
be pure noise. These trips should be quarantined for regulatory audit.

## Finding 6 — Zone 265 ("Outside of NYC"): Anomalous Tip Rate

**Confidence: High**

**Evidence:**
- Zone 265 is the top anomaly in `zone_destination_pattern` (delta_norm = 8.07, severity = high)
- `_d_dropoff_avg_tip` contributes 80.3% of anomaly score; the repair requires only correcting
  that single dimension, confirming it is the sole driver
- Zone 265 dropoff trips show average tip = **$0.506** (0 = no tip in other zones; this zone
  has avg tip_pct = 0.51, almost double the citywide credit-card average of ~0.22)
- The zone has an anomalous payment mix: 4,370 "Dispute" records (31% of all disputes in the
  dataset), 4,943 "Unknown" payment records, and 864 "No charge" trips — all
  disproportionately high for a single zone
- Average fare is $70.77, average distance 16.96 miles — consistent with airport-level or
  outer-borough long trips

**Interpretation:**
"Outside of NYC" is a legitimate zone for intercity trips (e.g. to airports in New Jersey,
Connecticut, or Long Island destinations beyond city limits). The high tip rate is real —
longer, high-value trips attract proportionally larger tips. The concentration of "Dispute" and
"Unknown" payment codes here is a genuine concern: intercity trips may have higher billing
disputes and payment method ambiguity (corporate accounts, prepaid fares). This is a
legitimate operational signal, not a data error, but warrants rate-structure review.

## Finding 7 — Invalid Rate Code 99 (103 Trips)

**Confidence: Very High**

**Evidence:**
- Valid TLC RatecodeIDs are 1–6. **103 trips** carry `RatecodeID = 99`
- These 99-coded trips have: min fare $2.50, max $52.00, mean $9.95 — broadly normal values
- The `rate_codes` line maps 99 to no named code; it appears in the zone 264 ("N/A" / unknown)
  cluster at a higher-than-expected rate
- 103 trips out of 7.5M = 0.00136% — tiny volume but definitively invalid

**Interpretation:**
Rate code 99 is an undefined/error code in the TLC data dictionary. These 103 trips had
their meter code set to an invalid value, likely due to driver input error or firmware bug.
They should be reclassified to Standard rate (1) for analysis purposes or excluded.

## Finding 8 — Temporal Outliers: 370 Trips Outside January 2019

**Confidence: Very High**

**Evidence:**
- This dataset covers January 2019. The `pickup_date` profile reveals 51 distinct dates.
- **343 trips** have pickup dates **before 2019-01-01** (all from Vendor 2 / VeriFone):
  - 2018-12-31: 282 trips
  - 2018-12-30: 7 trips
  - 2008-12-31: 17 trips (ten years in the past)
  - 2009-01-01: 37 trips (ten years in the past)
- **27 trips** have pickup dates **after 2019-01-31** (all from Vendor 2):
  - 2019-02-01: 61 trips; 2019-02-02 through 2019-09-03: scattered 1–3 trips each
- The 54 trips dated 2008-12-31 and 2009-01-01 are a decade out of range — almost certainly
  an epoch/timestamp overflow or Y2K-style encoding error in Vendor 2's system

**Interpretation:**
Vendor 2 (VeriFone) has two temporal anomaly types: (1) ~350 trips on Dec 30–31 2018 that
are likely genuine late-December trips included due to a midnight-boundary processing overlap,
and (2) 54 trips showing 2008–2009 dates that are definitively wrong — a firmware or clock
synchronization failure. The future-dated trips (post Feb 2019) are probably a timezone or
buffer-flush issue. All should be excluded from January 2019 analysis.

## Zone-Level Structural Anomalies (Lower Priority)

### Airports (JFK Zone 132, LaGuardia Zone 138)
Both airports are confirmed anomalies in `zone_activity_pattern` (delta_norm 6.37 and 5.54)
driven by high total fare and average distance — these are legitimate operational outliers, not
data errors. JFK avg fare = $46.70, avg distance = 16.27 miles; LaGuardia avg fare = $30.56.
**Confidence: High (intentional, not data error)**

### Ghost Zones (103 — Governors Island, 110 — Great Kills Park)
These zones have `trip_count = 0`, `total_fare = 0`, `n_vendors = 0` across all dimensions.
They are anomalous because the population model expects some minimum activity. These are
genuinely inaccessible or restricted zones with no taxi service. Anomaly driven entirely by
`_d_n_vendors` (delta = -4.09, 56% contribution). **Confidence: Very High (structural absence)**

### Staten Island Remote Zones (5, 44, 84, 204)
Charleston/Tottenville (44), Arden Heights (5), Rossville/Woodrow (204), and
Eltingville/Annadale/Prince's Bay (84) are anomalous in both patterns due to extreme average
distance (25–32 miles), high average fare ($74–$85), and very low trip volume (27–95 trips
each). These represent rare but legitimate long inter-borough trips. A single monopoly vendor
(n_vendors = 1) services some of these zones. **Confidence: High (genuine operational outlier)**

## Regime Change: 2019-02-20 Tip Percentage Shift

**Confidence: Medium**

The `zone_activity_pattern` shows a single regime change at **2019-02-19** (magnitude 4.63,
threshold 4.05). The dominant dimension change is `_d_avg_tip_pct` (+3.47 sigma shift).
A similar shift appears in `zone_destination_pattern` at **2019-08-27** on `_d_dropoff_avg_tip`
(+3.93 sigma), though that timestamp is far outside the January 2019 data window and likely
reflects an artifact of the temporal slicing against the broader calibration history.

The February 2019 shift likely reflects NYC's congestion surcharge rollout or a change in
how tip prompts were presented on Vendor 2 card readers. It does not represent a data
integrity problem but is a real behavioral change. **The "future" regime change (Aug 2019)
should be disregarded** — it falls outside the data's valid range and is a calibration artifact
from the sphere's multi-year temporal index spanning 2008–2088 timestamps.

---

## Temporal Index Anomaly: Timestamps 2008–2088

**Confidence: Very High**

Throughout temporal analysis, all 18 zone-activity slices span
`first_timestamp = 2008-12-30T23:00:00` to `last_timestamp = 2088-01-20T23:00:00`. This
69-year window is not a reflection of real data — it corresponds to the temporal calibration
history stored in the sphere including the anomalous 2008–2009 trips and likely placeholder
or test slices. Drift and trajectory results derived from this index should be interpreted
with caution: the displacement ratios (0.06–0.52) and "arch/v-shape" trajectories detected
are meaningful in relative terms but their timestamps do not correspond to real operational
periods.

## Methodology Notes

- Navigation performed via hypertopos GDS sphere `nyc_taxi_2019_jan`
- Anomaly detection uses geometric delta vectors against population mu/sigma/theta calibration
- Conformal p-values confirm statistical significance (zone 237: p=0.0038, zone 265: p=0.0038)
- All property ranges cross-validated against NYC TLC data dictionary for January 2019
- Temporal regime change detection used n_regimes=5; only 1 significant changepoint found
  within the January 2019 window
- Trip-level drill-downs used `aggregate()` with `event_filters` to count records outside
  valid domain ranges
