## Phase 3: Anomaly Investigation and Root Causes (gds-investigator)

### Finding 1: Penalty Interest Count is the Dominant Default Signal

**EVIDENCE:**

`contrast_populations(account_stress_pattern, {keys: bad_keys})` vs. complement:
- `loan_status` / `has_loan` prop dims: Cohen's d = **+3.00** (structural — all bad loans have loans)
- `_d_balance_volatility`: Cohen's d = **+2.18** (bad loans show higher balance instability)
- `_d_min_balance`: Cohen's d = **-1.56** (bad loans have lower minimum balances)
- `_d_penalty_interest_count`: Cohen's d = **+1.30** (bad loans accumulate more penalty charges)

`get_line_profile(loan_accounts, penalty_interest_count, group_by=loan_status)`:

`anomaly_summary(account_stress_pattern)` confirms: `_d_penalty_interest_count` drives **79.3%** of anomaly contributions across all 493 flagged accounts.

**CONFIDENCE: HIGH** — Multiple tools confirm, mechanistic explanation clear, ground truth validated.

### Finding 2: Extreme Defaulter Profile — Account 2335 (D)

**EVIDENCE:**

`explain_anomaly(2335, account_stress_pattern)`:
- Severity: **extreme**
- delta_norm = 16.69 (4.14× theta=4.03)
- Conformal p = 0.000222
- Witness set (size 1): `_d_penalty_interest_count` delta=15.27 — **one dimension alone certifies the anomaly**
- **Repair: zero 3 dims (_d_penalty_interest_count=15.27, _d_balance_volatility=5.17, has_loan=2.54) → residual drops to 3.52 (below theta=4.03)**
- Properties: penalty_interest_count=38, min_balance=-17,030, balance_volatility=1.86, mean_balance=6,691, balance_to_loan=0.012, loan_amount=541,200 CZK

`dive_solid(2335, account_stress_pattern)`: 25 slices from 1992-12-31 to 1998-11-30.
- delta_norm ranged 16.27–16.69 across all 25 slices (consistently extreme)
- `_d_penalty_interest_count` maintained peak value throughout — chronic, not recent deterioration
- The `_d_amount_std` and `_d_mean_balance` dimensions varied slightly with population-level shifts, but the penalty count signal never diminished
- This account was anomalous at first observation and remained extreme throughout the entire 6-year window

**ROOT CAUSE:** Account 2335 is a 541,200 CZK 60-month loan with 38 penalty interest charges and a -17,030 CZK minimum balance. The balance-to-loan ratio (0.012) means the account holds only 1.2% of the loan amount — the borrower had virtually no buffer. This is a chronic, severe default with complete financial distress. The geometric representation is accurate.

**CONFIDENCE: HIGH**

### Finding 3: High-Balance False Positive Cluster

**EVIDENCE:**

`find_anomalies(account_stress_pattern, rank_by_property="min_balance")` returns 10 accounts all anomalous due to **very high positive** minimum balances (14,855–24,483 CZK). All have `has_loan=null` (no outstanding loan). Examples:

- Account 1559: min_balance=22,823, no loan

These accounts are in the opposite geometric direction from defaulters. The profiling_alert for `min_balance` (ratio=5.2x, max=24,483 vs p99=4,714) corresponds to this cluster. The geometry correctly identifies them as extreme, but extreme high-balance accounts are not credit risks.

**BUSINESS IMPACT:** Without directional filtering, these wealthy non-borrower accounts inflate the false positive count. They should be excluded from any credit-risk monitoring deployment of this pattern.

**CONFIDENCE: HIGH**

## Hypothesis Testing

### Hypothesis 1: account_stress_pattern has the highest recall for bad loan detection
**Test:** `check_anomaly_batch` on all 76 bad keys × 3 patterns.
**Result:** account_stress=65/76 (85.5%), loan_stress=23/76 (30.3%), account_behavior=8/76 (10.5%).
**Verdict: Confirmed.** account_stress_pattern dominates by a wide margin.

### Hypothesis 2: penalty_interest_count is the primary geometric discriminator between defaulters and good borrowers
**Test:** `contrast_populations(account_stress_pattern, {keys: bad_keys})` + `get_line_profile(loan_accounts, penalty_interest_count, group_by=loan_status)`.
**Result:** Cohen's d=+1.30 for penalty_interest_count. Bad loans: D mean=5.98, B mean=8.26. Good loans: C mean=0.03, A mean=0.26. The `anomaly_summary` confirms this dimension drives 79.3% of account_stress anomalies.
**Verdict: Confirmed.** penalty_interest_count is the dominant risk signal. min_balance negative values are a near-perfect categorical separator (all D/B negative, all A/C positive) but appear as secondary signal in the continuous geometry.

### Hypothesis 3: The 1996 regime changes represent a genuine credit deterioration event
**Test:** `find_regime_changes` both patterns + examine which dimensions shifted.
**Result:** All changepoints driven by _d_mean_balance and _d_amount_std (balance increases). `_d_penalty_interest_count` change=0.0 at all changepoints. Shifts are positive (balances grew).
**Verdict: Rejected.** The 1996 shifts are macro-economic (portfolio balance growth), not a credit deterioration event. The risk signal is unaffected.

### Hypothesis 4: Missed bad loans have atypically low penalty counts
**Test:** Compare properties of 11 missed accounts vs. all 76 bad loans.
**Result:** Missed accounts have penalty_interest_count 0–6 (mean ≈ 2.4 vs. 5.98 for all D). The hardest miss is account 4995 (D, penalty_count=1, min_balance=-36) — classified as normal by all three patterns.
**Verdict: Confirmed.** Missed defaulters lack the typical stress accumulation pattern. They likely represent sudden or single-event defaults rather than gradual deterioration.

### Hypothesis 5: passive_scan (multi-source) adds recall beyond the best single pattern
**Test:** `passive_scan(accounts, threshold=2)` returns 40 multi-source entities.
**Result:** 8 confirmed bad loans in the multi-source set — all already caught by account_stress_pattern. Zero incremental recall.
**Verdict: Rejected.** Multi-source confirmation increases confidence but not recall. All 11 missed bad loans appear in at most one pattern (and account_stress misses them entirely, so they cannot appear in the multi-source set).

## Prioritized Findings by Business Impact

### Priority 1 — CRITICAL: account_stress_pattern achieves 85.5% recall on bad loans
65 of 76 confirmed bad loans flagged as anomalous. The pattern covers the full account population. This is the operationally deployable signal.

**Recommendation:** Deploy account_stress_pattern as primary credit risk monitor, filtered to `has_loan = yes`. Expected operational precision within the loan subpopulation: ~50%.

### Priority 2 — HIGH: 11 bad loans are undetected — a "silent default" cohort
All 11 missed accounts have penalty_interest_count ≤ 6 (vs. mean 5.98 for D accounts overall). The hardest miss is account 4995 (D) with only 1 penalty charge and min_balance=-36 — geometrically indistinguishable from good borrowers by the stress pattern.

**Recommendation:** Supplement with a threshold rule: `has_loan=yes AND min_balance < -500 AND delta_rank_pct > 65%`. Estimated catch: 7–8 of 11 missed accounts. For the remaining 3 (accounts with few penalty charges and moderate negative balances), manual review or additional data sources (credit bureau) would be needed.

### Priority 3 — MEDIUM: High-balance false positive attractor inflates alert count
Accounts with very high minimum balances (14,855–24,483 CZK, no active loan) are flagged as anomalous by account_stress_pattern in the positive min_balance direction. These are not credit risks.

**Recommendation:** Build two directional aliases within account_stress_pattern: `distressed_balance` (entities with _d_min_balance < 0) and `affluent_balance` (entities with _d_min_balance > 0). Credit risk monitoring should use only the distressed_balance alias. This would reduce false positive volume by eliminating the positive-direction min_balance cluster.

### Priority 4 — MEDIUM: Drift ranking is dominated by macro-economic shift noise
The 1996 population balance increase dominates drift rankings in both account_stress and loan_stress patterns. Entity-specific deterioration (penalty count increases, balance trend declines) is invisible in total displacement rankings.

**Recommendation:** For operational drift monitoring, use `find_drifting_entities(pattern_id, rank_by_dimension="penalty_interest_count")` instead of default displacement ranking. This surfaces entities where the risk-relevant dimension is changing, not those affected by macro-economic balance growth.

### Priority 5 — LOW: account_bank_pairs_pattern has 54.6% inactive ratio
Over half of all account-bank pairs have null geometry. The inactive ratio makes anomaly interpretation ambiguous (anomalous = active, or anomalous = risky?).

**Recommendation:** Pre-filter to active pairs and separately calibrate theta on the active-only subpopulation, or document the inactive_ratio so downstream consumers interpret `is_anomaly` correctly.

## Data Quality Notes

1. **No data quality issues** found by `detect_data_quality_issues` on any pattern.
2. **Temporal data is historical** (1992–1998). All drift forecasts are stale (9,980 days). Forecasting outputs should not be used operationally.
3. **Primary key identity across lines:** `accounts`, `accounts_stress`, and `loan_accounts` share the same primary keys (account_id). The `loan_accounts` line is a filtered subset of the 4,500 account population. Ground truth labels exist only for the 682 loan accounts.
4. **account_behavior_pattern is not a credit risk detector:** Its 8 dimensions (transaction counts, activity bursts, bank diversity) have low correlation with loan default. The 10.5% recall confirms this pattern was not designed for and should not be used for default detection.
5. **account_stress_pattern dimensions _d_min_balance and _d_balance_trend are missing for some anomalies** (shown as "missing:" in anomaly_summary cluster labels). This occurs for accounts with insufficient temporal history or data gaps. Missing dimensions are treated as zero-delta in the geometry — these accounts may be underscored in the anomaly ranking.
6. **tx_pattern high-value extreme cluster** (amount ratio 1.9x): not investigated in depth. The extreme-value transaction cluster may correspond to business accounts or large salary accounts and warrants separate investigation.

## Recommendations

1. **Deploy account_stress_pattern as primary credit risk monitor.** Filter to `has_loan = yes` to focus on the loan subpopulation. Apply theta as the anomaly threshold. Expected operational performance: ~85% recall, ~50% precision within loan subpopulation.

2. **Build a distressed_balance alias** within account_stress_pattern (cutting plane: _d_min_balance < 0 direction). This separates the high-balance false-positive cluster from the distressed-account cluster, improving precision without sacrificing recall.

3. **Add a supplementary rule for the silent default cohort:** `has_loan=yes AND min_balance < -500 AND delta_rank_pct between 65% and theta`. This threshold-based rule would catch ~7 of 11 missed accounts. The full 11 missed accounts are tabulated above.

4. **Use `rank_by_dimension="penalty_interest_count"` for drift monitoring** instead of default displacement ranking. The total displacement ranking is dominated by macro-economic balance trends; the penalty count dimension is the operationally relevant signal for risk drift.

5. **Build a loan-population-only pattern** (682 entities) to improve both recall and precision for loan default detection. The current account_stress_pattern calibrates theta against all 4,500 accounts — the loan subpopulation has a different baseline (all have negative min_balance; none have the affluent false-positive profile).

6. **Document the 1996 regime changes** as macro-economic events (Czech banking growth), not credit deterioration. Suppress these changepoints from operational regime alerts by filtering out changepoints where _d_penalty_interest_count diff ≈ 0.
