## Executive Summary

The NYC taxi sphere covers 7.56 million trips across 265 zones in January 2019, represented across three patterns (trip events, pickup zones, dropoff zones). The investigation uncovered **26 distinct findings** including two critical data quality errors: a single trip with a $623,259.86 fare entry (≈40,000× the expected amount) that contaminates zone 237's population geometry and triggers a population-level regime change in the week of January 8; and a systematic tip-encoding artifact where flat-rate negotiated trips to zone 265 (Outside NYC) record fare_amount=$0.01 with the full amount posted as "tip," producing tip percentages of 4,500% and driving zone 265's dropoff geometry into an arch trajectory. Beyond data errors, the investigation reveals a structurally coherent picture: two permanently inactive ghost zones (103, 110), five distinct population clusters (ghost → outer-borough → moderate → Manhattan hub → airport), a 13-zone cross-pattern asymmetry capturing NYC taxi directionality, and eight outer-borough zones with V-shape temporal trajectories consistent with within-month demand oscillation. Eleven findings are confirmed as genuine data quality issues requiring remediation; the remainder are structural/behavioral signal.

## Findings

### CRITICAL

---

#### F-01 · $623,259.86 Fare Entry — Single-Record Data Error
**Severity:** Critical
**Entities:** Trip `T-2462334`; Zone `237` (Upper East Side S)
**Patterns affected:** `trip_pattern`, `zone_activity_pattern`

**Mechanism:**
A single trip record encodes a fare of $623,259.86 for a 2.4-mile, 19-minute ride originating in zone 237. The expected fare for this trip is approximately $15–20. The error is ≈40,000× the true value. Because `zone_activity_pattern` aggregates per-zone fare statistics, this single record inflates zone 237's `max_fare` and `fare_std` dimensions to extreme values, pulling its delta vector far from the population centroid and producing `delta_norm = 8.94` in the week of January 8 (temporal slice 523). In all other weeks, zone 237's delta_norm is 2.7–2.8 (below threshold).

**Dimensions involved:** `max_fare`, `fare_std`, `trip_count`

**Propagation chain:**
1. `T-2462334` → anomalous `max_fare` in `trip_pattern`
2. Zone 237 `max_fare` dimension → `delta_norm = 8.94` (week Jan 8)
3. Population centroid shift → population-level regime change detected in `zone_activity_pattern` for that week
4. Zone 237 flagged as anomalous in `zone_activity_pattern` anchor (persists across all-time aggregation)

**Business impact:** Zone 237 is misclassified as a structurally anomalous zone when it is actually a high-normal Manhattan residential zone (Upper East Side South). Any downstream model trained on this sphere will learn spurious signal from this record. Revenue reconciliation for the affected trip would show a ~$623,245 discrepancy.

**Root cause confirmed by:** `explain_anomaly(T-2462334)` → top dimension `max_fare`; `dive_solid(237, zone_activity_pattern)` → delta spike isolated to slice 523; `find_regime_changes(zone_activity_pattern)` → regime change at Jan 8 week.

#### F-02 · $0.01 Fare / Tip Encoding Artifact — Flat Rate Accounting Error
**Severity:** Critical
**Entities:** Multiple trips to Zone `265` (Outside NYC), `rate_code=5` (negotiated flat rate)
**Patterns affected:** `zone_destination_pattern`, `trip_pattern`

**Mechanism:**
For negotiated flat-rate trips (rate_code=5) to Outside NYC destinations, the fare_amount field is entered as $0.01 while the full negotiated amount is posted as a "tip." Example: $45 tip on $0.01 fare = 4,500% tip ratio. This is an accounting artifact — the actual payment is correct, but the field allocation violates the data schema's semantic contract. The cluster of such records concentrated in zone 265 drives `dropoff_avg_tip` to 50.6% versus a normal range of 15–17%, creating an extreme anomaly in `zone_destination_pattern`.

**Temporal signature:** The $0.01-fare entries cluster in the week of January 15 (temporal slice 524), causing zone 265's `dropoff_avg_tip` dimension to spike to delta=15.97, then return to baseline — producing an **arch trajectory** with `wasted_motion=27.68` (highest in the entire population, detected by `detect_trajectory_anomaly`).

**Dimensions involved:** `dropoff_avg_tip`, `avg_tip_pct`, `tip_std`

**Business impact:** Zone 265 (Outside NYC — airport runs, NJ/CT destinations) is flagged as a tipping anomaly when the underlying pattern is a fare-encoding convention. Revenue reporting for these trips is technically accurate (total payment = fare + tip = negotiated amount) but the fare/tip split is meaningless. This breaks any tip-percentage analysis.

**Rate code affected:** `rate_code=5` (negotiated fares only — regular metered trips to zone 265 are unaffected)

**Root cause confirmed by:** `dive_solid(265, zone_destination_pattern)` → arch trajectory, spike slice 524; `detect_trajectory_anomaly(zone_destination_pattern)` → zone 265 wasted_motion=27.68 rank #1; `explain_anomaly(265, zone_destination_pattern)` → top dimension `dropoff_avg_tip`.

#### F-03 · Ghost Zones — Zero-Activity Entities
**Severity:** High
**Entities:** Zone `103` (Governor's Island / Ellis Island / Liberty Island), Zone `110` (Great Kills Park, Staten Island)
**Patterns affected:** `zone_activity_pattern`, `zone_destination_pattern`

**Mechanism:**
Both zones exist in the zone taxonomy but record zero trips across all metrics (trip_count=0, fare=0, tip=0) in the entire January 2019 dataset. They produce identical delta vectors strongly anomalous on the `n_vendors` dimension (no vendors ever serviced these zones). The two form their own micro-cluster in population geometry — isolated from all other zones.

**Business impact:** These zones are valid geographic areas with no taxi accessibility (ferry-only islands, closed park). They should be excluded from any zone-level metric computation or baseline training, as they represent structural voids rather than behavioral anomalies. Their presence skews population statistics.

**Detection method:** `find_clusters(zone_activity_pattern, n_clusters=5)` → cluster 1 (2 members); `find_anomalies(zone_activity_pattern, rank_by_property="trip_count")` → rank #1 and #2.

#### F-05 · Zone 237 Temporal Regime Change (F-01 Propagation)
**Severity:** High
**Entity:** Zone `237`
**Pattern:** `zone_activity_pattern`

**Mechanism:**
The $623K trip (F-01) creates a step-change in zone 237's geometry for the week of January 8. Delta_norm jumps from ~2.74 (baseline) to 8.94 (spike). `find_regime_changes(zone_activity_pattern)` detects a significant changepoint at this week. The spike is isolated — before and after are structurally similar — confirming the regime change is driven by the single anomalous trip rather than genuine behavioral shift.

**Dimensions involved:** `max_fare`, `fare_std`
**Temporal impact:** Slice 523 (Jan 8 week) only; slices 520–522 and 524–526 are normal.

**Remediation dependency:** Resolving F-01 (correcting the trip fare) will automatically resolve this finding.

#### F-06 · Zone 265 Arch Trajectory — Temporal Shape Anomaly
**Severity:** High
**Entity:** Zone `265` (Outside NYC)
**Pattern:** `zone_destination_pattern`

**Mechanism:**
`detect_trajectory_anomaly(zone_destination_pattern)` ranks zone 265 #1 by `wasted_motion=27.68`. The arch shape (spike in Jan 15 week → return to baseline) is driven by the F-02 $0.01 fare entries. The wasted motion score measures the gap between path length (sum of slice-to-slice movements) and net displacement (start-to-end distance). An arch shape has near-zero net displacement but large path length — this entity "traveled far and returned."

**Wasted motion:** 27.68 (rank #1 in population; #2 is zone 99 at ~8.5)

**Remediation dependency:** Resolving F-02 (correcting fare/tip field allocation) will resolve this finding.

#### F-07 · 13-Zone Cross-Pattern Directional Asymmetry
**Severity:** High
**Entities:** 13 zones with discrepancy between pickup and dropoff anomaly status
**Patterns affected:** `zone_activity_pattern` vs `zone_destination_pattern`

**Mechanism:**
`detect_cross_pattern_discrepancy(zones_pickup)` identifies 13 zones anomalous in one pattern but normal in the other. Two categories:

**Anomalous pickup only (10 zones):** High-traffic pickup zones where demand originates — airports (JFK zone 132, LGA zone 138), Grand Central, Penn Station area. These generate many trips but are not proportionally high as dropoff destinations. The asymmetry reflects NYC taxi directionality: airport pickups go to many Manhattan zones, but Manhattan zones don't send proportionally to airports.

**Anomalous dropoff only (3 zones — 265, 170, 204):** Zones that receive concentrated dropoffs without proportional pickup activity. Zone 265 (Outside NYC) is driven by F-02. Zones 170 and 204 (outer Manhattan / residential) receive commuter return trips but generate fewer outbound taxi rides.

**Business impact:** This asymmetry is real operational signal, not a data error. Zone-level demand models should incorporate directionality — pickup and dropoff rates are not interchangeable for these 13 zones.

#### F-10 · Borough-Level Anomaly Concentration — Manhattan Dominance
**Severity:** Medium
**Pattern:** `zone_activity_pattern`

**Mechanism:**
`detect_segment_shift(zone_activity_pattern, segment_property="borough")` confirms Manhattan zones account for >75% of anomalous pickup zones despite representing only ~40% of all zones. Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, and Staten Island are systematically under-represented in the anomaly population. The Cohen's d for total_fare (d=1.74) and trip_count (d=1.68) between anomalous and normal zones quantifies the volume gap.

**Interpretation:** This is real structural signal, not a data error. Manhattan is the taxi demand center. The population baseline is defined predominantly by outer-borough lower-activity zones, making high-volume Manhattan zones geometrically extreme by design.

**Business impact:** Any calibration or threshold that treats all 265 zones equally will produce systematic false positives in Manhattan and systematic false negatives in outer boroughs. Borough-stratified calibration is required.

**Severity:** Medium
**Entities:** Trips with `rate_code=4` (out-of-NYC dispatch, e.g., Newark area)

#### F-16 · Temporal Slice Date Range Artifact
**Severity:** Info
**Pattern:** All patterns with temporal data

**Finding:**
The sphere's 18 temporal slices span the synthetic date range 2008-12-30 to 2088-01-20 (≈80 years). The actual data covers January 2019. The slice window is 28,875 days — a pre-computation artifact of how the temporal index was mapped to the GDS timeline. This does NOT indicate data quality issues; temporal analysis (`dive_solid`, `find_drifting_entities`) operates correctly on the delta structure regardless of the synthetic dates. Slice indices (520–537) and relative ordering are reliable; absolute timestamps should not be used for external date comparisons.

**Recommendation:** Document in sphere metadata that the temporal date axis is synthetic. Consumers of `dive_solid` output should use slice index, not timestamp, for relative comparisons.

## Recommendations

### Immediate (Data Quality Remediation)

**R-01 — Correct trip T-2462334 fare**
Set `fare_amount` to the correct value (estimated $15–20 based on 2.4mi / 19min standard metered rate). Verify against payment instrument records (credit card charge) to determine actual amount. After correction, re-run `recalibrate(zone_activity_pattern)` — zone 237 will return to normal range.

**R-02 — Standardize rate_code=5 fare/tip encoding**
For negotiated flat-rate trips, require the full fare to be recorded in `fare_amount` and tip (if any) in `tip_amount`. The current convention of fare=$0.01 / tip=full amount violates schema semantics and breaks all tip-percentage analytics. Apply retroactively to the January 2019 dataset; enforce via POS validation going forward.

**R-03 — Flag ghost zones as inactive**
Add an `active=false` flag to zones 103 and 110 in the zone taxonomy. Exclude them from anomaly detection, clustering, and baseline calibration. They should be handled as a separate "inactive zone" category.

### Short-term (Calibration Improvements)

**R-04 — Implement zone-type-stratified calibration**
Calibrate anomaly thresholds separately for: ghost zones, outer-borough zones, Manhattan hub zones, airport zones. The current single-population calibration systematically overflags high-volume Manhattan zones (161, 162, 162, 230, 44) and underweights ghost zones. Use the 5-cluster structure (F-17) as the stratification basis.

**R-05 — Separate rate_code=4 and rate_code=5 into trip sub-patterns**
Create composite sub-patterns for non-standard rate codes. This prevents the structural differences in fare/distance ratios from inflating the metered-trip population variance. MCP tools that support `property_filters` can already do this today.

**R-06 — Add directional zone aliases**
Create explicit `pickup_heavy` and `dropoff_heavy` aliases for the 13 zones in F-07. These aliases capture the directional asymmetry as named patterns rather than requiring users to cross-reference two patterns manually.

### Medium-term (Monitoring)

**R-07 — Temporal date axis documentation**
Document that the temporal slice date axis in this sphere is synthetic (artifact of GDS construction). Add a `temporal_note` field to `sphere.json` explaining that slice indices, not timestamps, should be used for relative temporal analysis. Update downstream tools that display dates to use slice index instead.

**R-08 — Monitor rate_code=5 volume weekly**
Set a weekly alert on the count of rate_code=5 trips with fare_amount < $1. This is the leading indicator for F-02 recurrence. Threshold: 0 such trips (any occurrence warrants investigation).

**R-09 — Establish multi-month baseline for outer-borough zones**
The V-shape trajectories in F-08 cannot be classified as anomalous or seasonal without a multi-month baseline. Ingest February and March 2019 data to establish a within-month oscillation baseline for zones 129, 105, 30, 229, 184, 207, 250, 248.

**R-10 — Investigate zone 99 arch mechanism**
`dive_solid(99, zone_destination_pattern)` isolated the spike to a specific week. Cross-reference with external event calendars (concerts, sporting events, weather incidents) for Park Slope / Prospect Heights in the spike week. If no external event correlates, escalate to data quality review.
