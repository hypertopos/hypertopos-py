## 3. Hypotheses, Tests, and Results

### Hypothesis 1: account_stress_pattern anomalies predict loan default (recall)

**Hypothesis:** Geometric anomalies in the stress pattern (penalty interest, balance volatility, min balance) are concentrated in B+D accounts.

**Test:** `check_anomaly_batch` for all 76 default accounts against `account_stress_pattern`.

**Results:**
- Status D (45 accounts): **40 flagged as anomalous** → recall = 88.9%
- Status B (31 accounts): **25 flagged as anomalous** → recall = 80.6%
- **Combined default recall (B+D): 65 of 76 = 85.5%**
- False positives: 493 total anomalies − 65 true positives = 428 from non-default accounts (86.8% FP rate)

**Verdict: CONFIRMED.** The `account_stress_pattern` has high recall for defaults, but low precision (13.2%) because 493 total anomalies include many stressed non-defaulted accounts.

**Why FPs matter:** Penalty interest counts and balance volatility are elevated across a wider set of accounts (including C-status borrowers still running) — the pattern picks up financial stress broadly, not just eventual default. This is useful for risk triage but not for binary classification.

**Non-recalled defaults (11 B+D accounts not flagged):**
- Status D missed: `3084` (85th pct), `9052` (79th pct), `6715` (76th pct), `2051` (73rd pct), `4995` (65th pct)
- Status B missed: `1527`, `6034`, `1787`, `6400`, `5066`, `8073`
- These are accounts with relatively moderate volatility and penalty counts — they defaulted via balance deterioration rather than excessive overdraft behavior.

### Hypothesis 2: loan_stress_pattern provides more precise (but lower recall) default detection

**Hypothesis:** The loan-specific pattern with balance_to_loan and income_coverage adds precision at cost of recall.

**Test:** `check_anomaly_batch` for all 76 default accounts against `loan_stress_pattern`.

**Results:**
- Status D (45 accounts): **13 flagged** → recall = 28.9%  (12 at theta, +1 just above threshold shown in data)
- Status B (31 accounts): **11 flagged** → recall = 35.5%
- **Combined default recall (B+D): 24 of 76 = 31.6%**
- Total anomalies in pattern: 68 of 682 → 24 TP → precision = 35.3%, FP rate = 64.7%

**Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED.** The loan_stress_pattern has lower recall but comparable precision to account_stress_pattern. The improvement in precision is modest (35% vs 13%) while recall drops substantially (32% vs 86%).

**Root cause of low recall in loan_stress_pattern:**
The pattern uses 8 dimensions (penalty_interest_count, balance_volatility, min_balance, income_coverage, balance_to_loan, amount_std, mean_balance, balance_trend). Many B+D accounts score in the 50–90th percentile — elevated but sub-threshold. The theta_norm of 3.83 is tight given the continuous geometry.

**Two distinct anomaly archetypes in loan_stress_pattern:**
1. **High-penalty cluster** (accounts 2335, 7418, 5927, 3678, 4081): Driven by extreme `_d_penalty_interest_count` (delta 6–10×) + high balance volatility. All are B or D status. These are catastrophic failures.
2. **Ultra-low-balance-to-loan cluster** (accounts 378, 4681, 3804, 9640, 8330, 10711): Driven by very high `_d_min_balance` (delta 7–9×) and high `_d_income_coverage` — accounts with tiny loans relative to their wealth. These are predominantly C-status (performing). **These are false positives:** geometrically extreme but not at risk.

### Hypothesis 3: account_behavior_pattern anomalies represent dormant/low-activity accounts, not defaults

**Hypothesis:** The 225 anomalies in `account_behavior_pattern` are dormant accounts (very few transactions, minimal banking activity) rather than financially stressed accounts.

**Test:** Examined top anomalies — all show uniform delta pattern with large negative deltas on `_d_burst_monthly` (−3.36), `_d_n_categories` (−3.09), `_d_n_operations` (−3.05). Top account "182" has only tx_count=9, n_categories=1, n_operations=2, burst_monthly=1. The typical account has tx_count≈235, n_categories≈5.

**Results:** All 26 top anomalies show identical dimension loading: missing activity on all 8 behavioral dimensions. The clusters confirm this is one coherent group: accounts with only 9–30 transactions, 1 distinct bank, 1 transaction category, 2 operations, and 0 standing orders. These are deposit-only accounts with minimal usage, spread nationwide with no regional concentration.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED.** The 225 behavioral anomalies are entirely dormant/micro-activity accounts. Zero overlap with default risk signal.

**Why it matters:** These accounts may represent dormant savings accounts or accounts opened but barely used. They are false positives for any credit risk interpretation but are valid operational anomalies (e.g., inactive account review). The `low_engagement_accounts` alias captures 3,802 accounts near this boundary.

Additionally, 2 anomalies in the cluster labeled "excess edges" (accounts 655, 3882 and 2938, 3096) have positive deltas across all dimensions — these are hyper-active accounts with anomalously high burst counts, representing the opposite extreme.

### Hypothesis 4: Account-bank pairs anomalies identify high-volume, single-bank concentration risk

**Hypothesis:** Anomalies in `account_bank_pairs_pattern` reflect accounts with extreme transaction volume to a single bank counterparty, specifically the empty-string bank (internal transfers).

**Test:** `find_anomalies` on `account_bank_pairs_pattern`, `anomaly_summary`.

**Results:**
- 586 anomalous pairs out of 11,719 (5.0% = calibration boundary)
- All top anomalies have `bank=""` (empty string = internal/own-bank transactions)
- Driving dimensions: `_d_pair_sum_amount` (50.3%), `_d_pair_tx_count` (25.3%), `_d_pair_amount_std` (18.5%)
- Example: account 3786 has 424 transactions to internal bank, sum CZK 5.9M, std 15,864 — far exceeding population mean
- The inactive_ratio of 54.56% means most pairs have few/zero transactions (expected for sparse counterparty relationships)

**Verdict: CONFIRMED.** The anomalous pairs are high-volume internal-transfer accounts with unusually large single-counterparty concentration. The empty bank code likely denotes domestic own-bank or cash transactions. These are monitoring candidates for concentration risk or unusual cash flow patterns.

**Cross-reference with defaults:** Account 10266 (loan_status=B) appears in the top 15 bank-pair anomalies with 431 transactions to internal bank, sum CZK 4.3M — suggesting high-velocity accounts can also default.

### Hypothesis 5: Temporal regime shifts in loan population precede or correlate with default concentration

**Hypothesis:** The loan portfolio shows population-level geometry shifts (regime changes) at identifiable dates, consistent with macroeconomic stress periods.

**Test:** `find_regime_changes` on `account_stress_pattern` and `loan_stress_pattern`.

**Results:**
- **account_stress_pattern:** 3 regime changes detected:
  - 1996-03-15 (magnitude 0.340, threshold 0.280) — driven by mean_balance + amount_std shift
  - 1996-09-11 (magnitude 0.292)
  - 1997-03-10 (magnitude 0.288)
- **loan_stress_pattern:** 2 stronger regime changes:
  - 1996-03-15 (magnitude 0.530, threshold 0.406) — largest, driven by mean_balance + amount_std
  - 1996-09-11 (magnitude 0.421)

**Verdict: CONFIRMED.** Both populations show the same regime change dates (Q1 1996 and Q3 1996), with the loan population exhibiting larger centroid shifts (1.56× larger at Q1 1996). This is consistent with economic conditions in the Czech Republic in 1995-1996 (banking sector difficulties, currency turbulence). The `_d_penalty_interest_count` shows zero diff at all changepoints — the population-level penalty counts did not shift en masse, suggesting these were balance/income structural shifts rather than widespread overdraft events.

## 9. Data Quality Observations

1. **Dormant account cluster:** 225 accounts (5% of 4,500) have tx_count ≤ 30 and essentially no banking activity. These are structurally different from the main population and may warrant separate treatment.

2. **Empty bank code in transactions:** The top anomalies in `account_bank_pairs_pattern` all have `bank=""`. This is either a data encoding artifact (cash transactions or internal settlements coded as empty string) or a genuine counterparty type. It drives the highest anomaly scores in that pattern and merits data source review.

3. **Inactive pair ratio (54.56%):** Over half of account-bank pairs have near-zero activity. The high inactive_ratio in `account_bank_pairs_pattern` means the pattern operates on a sparse population — anomaly detection is comparing active pairs against a largely inactive base.

4. **Non-loan accounts with loan-like stress markers:** Account 3374 and 10656 (among others) show penalty counts of 29–33 without any loan product. These may represent unauthorized overdraft situations or data linkage issues.
