## Phase 3: Anomaly Investigation and Root Causes (gds-investigator)

### Finding 1: Penalty Interest Count is the Primary Anomaly Driver

**FINDING:** _d_penalty_interest_count drives 79.3% of anomaly contribution in account_stress_pattern and 32.4% in loan_stress_pattern.

**EVIDENCE:**
- contrast_populations(account_stress_pattern, all 76 bad loans): penalty_interest_count effect size = +1.30 (large)
- balance_volatility effect size = +2.18 (very large - strongest single dimension)
- min_balance effect size = -1.56 (bad loans have much more negative minimums)
- Raw mean penalty counts: B=8.26, D=5.98 vs A=0.26, C=0.030
- Most extreme: account 7418 (status B) = 40 penalty events, account 2335 (status D) = 38 events
- explain_anomaly(2335): severity=EXTREME, delta_norm=16.69, ratio=4.14, witness = _d_penalty_interest_count alone exceeds theta

**ROOT CAUSE:** dive_solid(2335) shows entity was anomalous from first slice (1992-12-31, delta_norm=16.53)
and remained above 16.29 across all 25 slices through 1998. Chronic distress, not event-triggered.

**CONFIDENCE:** HIGH - 3+ tools confirm, consistent across patterns.

### Finding 2: Balance Volatility is the Strongest Geometric Discriminator

**FINDING:** High balance volatility is the single strongest dimension separating defaulted from non-defaulted
loan accounts, with Cohen d = 2.23 in loan_stress_pattern.

**EVIDENCE:**
- contrast_populations(loan_stress_pattern): balance_volatility is #1 dimension by effect size (d=2.23)
- contrast_populations(account_stress_pattern): balance_volatility d=2.18 (strongest non-prop dimension)
- Raw mean volatility: B=0.704, D=0.707 vs A=0.388, C=0.387 - bad loans have 1.8x higher volatility
- Median volatility: bad loans=0.70 vs good loans=0.37

**CONFIDENCE:** HIGH - consistent very large effect across both patterns.

### Finding 3: Extreme Negative Minimum Balance Cluster

**FINDING:** A cluster of accounts has minimum balances as low as -41,126 CZK vs population median of +600 CZK.
This extreme cluster is partially absorbed by delta_norm normalization but clearly visible in raw profiles.

**EVIDENCE:**
- get_line_profile(accounts_stress, min_balance): min=-41,125.7, median=600, mean=431
- Bad loan groups ALL have negative mean min_balance: B mean=-4,444, D mean=-4,598 vs A=+761, C=+830
- min_balance effect size in loan_stress_pattern: d=-1.21 (large negative)
- Worst cases: account 5092 (min=-34,521), account 2305 (-23,685), account 2335 (-17,030), account 3678 (-15,009)
- balance_to_loan contrast effect size: d=-0.64 - bad loans have lower balance buffer

**CONFIDENCE:** HIGH - raw profiles and geometric dimensions consistent.

### Finding 4: account_behavior_pattern is an Inappropriate Default Detector

**FINDING:** account_behavior_pattern achieves only 10.5% recall because it measures transaction activity
(burst patterns, category diversity) rather than financial stress.

**EVIDENCE:**
- Anomaly summary top dimensions: _d_burst_monthly (22.9%), _d_n_categories (17.7%), _d_burst_weekly (16.2%) - activity, not stress
- Most anomaly clusters = missing all dimensions (dormant accounts) - structural absence, not risk
- contrast_populations(account_behavior_pattern, bad loans): top discriminators are loan_status prop (d=3.0) and has_loan prop (d=3.0)
  These are categorical flags, not behavioral signals.
- The 8 caught bad loans are anomalous for high transaction burst coincidentally, not because of financial distress

**CONFIDENCE:** HIGH - architectural mismatch fully explains low recall.

### Finding 5: Income Coverage is a Secondary Default Signal

**FINDING:** Defaulted loan accounts have lower income coverage ratios, but the effect is moderate (d=-0.53).

**EVIDENCE:**
- Raw means: income_coverage A=9.53, C=9.48, B=6.30, D=6.38 - bad loans 33% lower
- Full effect size ordering: balance_volatility (2.23) > penalty_count (1.36) > min_balance (1.21)
  > mean_balance (0.87) > balance_to_loan (0.64) > income_coverage (0.53) > balance_trend (0.41) > amount_std (0.14)

**CONFIDENCE:** MEDIUM - consistent direction, moderate effect size.

## Hypothesis Testing

### Hypothesis 1: Penalty interest count alone is sufficient to detect bad loans
**Test:** find_anomalies(account_stress_pattern, rank_by_property=penalty_interest_count)
**Result:** Top 20 include 7 confirmed bad loans and 13 non-loan stressed accounts with equally high counts
**Verdict:** PARTIALLY CONFIRMED - high penalty count is necessary but not sufficient; population contamination present

### Hypothesis 2: Bad loans show chronic distress from first observation (not event-triggered)
**Test:** dive_solid(2335) - check delta_norm across all 25 temporal slices
**Result:** delta_norm was 16.53 at slice 0 (1992-12-31), never dropped below 16.29 through 1998
**Verdict:** CONFIRMED - distress is chronic and pre-existing, independent of the 1996 macro regime change

### Hypothesis 3: Income coverage is the primary cause of loan default
**Test:** contrast_populations(loan_stress_pattern) - compare effect sizes
**Result:** income_coverage d=-0.53 (rank 6 of 8 dimensions); balance_volatility d=2.23 is the dominant signal
**Verdict:** REJECTED - income_coverage is a contributing factor only; balance_volatility and penalty_count are primary drivers

### Hypothesis 4: High-activity alias anomalies represent a different risk type from stress anomalies
**Test:** attract_boundary(high_activity_accounts, direction=in) - profile entity 68
**Result:** Entity 68 inside at delta_norm=5.00; anomaly driven by tx_burst/n_categories, not penalty/volatility
**Verdict:** CONFIRMED - high-activity anomalies are transaction behavioral outliers, distinct from financial stress

## Data Quality Notes

1. **Missing dimension edges:** Many anomaly clusters show missing: _d_mean_balance, _d_min_balance, _d_balance_trend
   - incomplete feature engineering for some accounts. Missing edges inflate delta_norm and may cause false positives.

2. **Stale temporal data:** All temporal slices end at 1998-11-30 (Berka dataset boundary). Drift forecasts carry low reliability.

3. **Inactive pairs at 54.6%:** account_bank_pairs_pattern is dominated by dormant relationships.

4. **Population contamination:** account_stress_pattern covers 4,500 accounts vs 682 with loans. A loan-only
   pattern would improve precision from 13.2% to an estimated 50%+.
