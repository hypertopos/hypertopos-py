## Executive Summary

The NYC Yellow Taxi January 2019 sphere covers 7,556,807 individual trips across 265 pickup/dropoff zones, modeled via three patterns (trip-level events, zone pickup geometry, zone dropoff geometry) and one alias. The investigation identified **21 findings** spanning data quality issues, structural zone anomalies, temporal trajectory anomalies, and cross-pattern discrepancies. The single most critical finding is a trip with fare $623,259.86 originating from zone 237 (Upper East Side South) — 12,000× the median fare — that poisons the entire zone's statistics and persists as a phantom signal across temporal slices. A secondary critical issue is corrupted temporal metadata (timestamps spanning 2008–2088 for January 2019 data), which invalidates all regime-change timestamps. Structurally, airports and high-volume Manhattan zones dominate the anomaly list for legitimate reasons; the genuinely suspicious signals are the extreme fare and tip outliers, two completely inactive zones, and an unclassified zone with no borough metadata.

### Finding 1 — CRITICAL: Zone 237 (Upper East Side South) — Single Trip with $623,259.86 Fare

**Affected entities:** Zone 237 (Manhattan, Yellow Zone, Upper East Side South)

**Detection path:** `sphere_overview` profiling alert → `find_anomalies(rank_by_property=max_fare)` → `explain_anomaly` → `dive_solid`

**Root cause:**
One individual trip recorded in the week of **2019-01-01 to 2019-01-08** carried a fare of **$623,259.86** — approximately 12,000× the median fare of $9.00 and far beyond the p99 of $52. This single record inflates the zone's `fare_std` to **1,085.31** (population p99 = $55) and drives `max_fare` to delta=7.64 (99.6th percentile). The spike is precisely localized:

- Slice 522 (2019-01-01): delta_norm=2.71 — **normal**
- Slice 523 (2019-01-08): delta_norm=**8.94** — SPIKE (fare_std=3.80, max_fare=7.64)
- Slice 524 (2019-01-15): delta_norm=2.82 — **returns to normal**

`explain_anomaly`: severity=HIGH, delta_norm=10.63, theta=5.35, ratio=1.99×. Witness: `_d_max_fare` alone (delta=7.64). Repair: zero 3 dims → residual=4.45 (just below theta). p-value=0.004.

The zone itself has 329,785 legitimate trips at avg fare $10.79 — it is a high-volume residential zone. The anomaly is entirely attributable to one erroneous record.

**False positive assessment:** NOT a false positive at the data quality level. The zone-level anomaly in `zone_activity_pattern` is inflated by the bad record. Remove the $623K trip and zone 237 drops below the anomaly threshold (repair residual=4.45 < theta=5.35).

**Recommended action:** Identify and quarantine trip ID with fare=$623,259.86, pickup week Jan 1–8, 2019. Likely a meter malfunction, test record, or data entry error. Flag for TLC data quality audit. Recalibrate `zone_activity_pattern` after removal.

### Finding 2 — CRITICAL: Corrupted Temporal Metadata — Timestamps Spanning 2008–2088

**Affected entities:** All 265 zones in both anchor patterns

**Detection path:** `find_drifting_entities` → `dive_solid` → temporal slice inspection

**Root cause:**
All temporal slices carry `first_timestamp = 2008-12-30` and `last_timestamp = 2088-01-20` — an 80-year span for a dataset covering January 2019. The `slice_window_days = 28,875` confirms the time axis encodes absolute day offsets from an epoch rather than actual event dates. The slice_index jumps from 526 (2019-01-29) directly to 4125 (2088-01-20), producing 10+ consecutive identical slices representing "dead zone" state for all post-January windows.

Consequences:
- The two detected regime changes (zone_activity_pattern: 2019-02-19; zone_destination_pattern: 2019-08-27) are **artifacts** of the data boundary, not real population shifts. They mark the transition from "active January data" to "empty future windows."
- `find_drifting_entities` displacements measure change over an 80-year synthetic window, not meaningful seasonal drift.
- Trajectory anomaly detection (arch/V-shape) operates on this synthetic time axis and may overstate non-linearity.

**False positive assessment:** All regime change findings and displacement rankings should be treated as structurally invalid until temporal metadata is corrected. Arch/V-shape trajectories (Finding 11) should be re-validated after a fix.

**Recommended action:** Rebuild the sphere's temporal index using actual event timestamps rather than absolute day offsets. Recalculate drift and regime change signals after fix.
