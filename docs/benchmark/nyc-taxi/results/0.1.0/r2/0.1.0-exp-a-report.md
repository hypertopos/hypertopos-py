# NYC Yellow Taxi — January 2019: Anomaly Report

**Sphere:** `benchmark/nyc-taxi/sphere/gds_nyc_taxi`
**Sphere ID:** `nyc_taxi_2019_jan`
**Dataset scope:** NYC Yellow Taxi, January 2019
**Total trips:** 7,556,807
**Exploration date:** 2026-03-31
**Method:** hypertopos GDS navigation (sphere_overview + detect_pattern chains + raw Arrow/Lance inspection)

---

## Executive Summary

Fourteen statistically distinct anomalous zones were identified across three patterns (trip_pattern, zone_activity_pattern, zone_destination_pattern). A single billing error of $623,259.86 dominates the zone-level geometry for Upper East Side South and inflates the population-wide fare standard deviation to 27x the 99th percentile. Beyond that single record, a consistent set of data quality and operational anomalies runs through the dataset: undocumented vendor entries, systematic zero-passenger logging by one vendor, extreme-distance trips with implausible physics, future-dated records spanning as far as year 2088, and a pre-release congestion surcharge present before its official NYC rollout.

## Anomaly Findings

### 1. Billing Error — $623,259.86 Fare (Upper East Side South, Zone 237)

**Confidence: Very High**

**Delta norm: 10.627 — highest in the dataset (#1 anomaly across all zones)**

Trip `T-2462334` was charged $623,259.86 for a 2.4-mile, 19-minute ride from Zone 237 (Upper East Side South, Manhattan) to Zone 90 on 2019-01-11 at 19:33. Rate code was standard (code 1), payment type was "no charge" (type 3), vendor was 1, and no tip was given. The fare is 11,985x the p99 fare of $52 for the same zone and roughly 10,000x the median fare for this trip length.

Impact on zone-level geometry:
- Zone 237 fare_std: **1,085.31** vs population p99 of 39.03 — a 27x exceedance
- Zone 237 max_fare: **$623,259.86** vs population p99 of $2,621 — a 238x exceedance
- Zone 237 is the #1 geometric anomaly (delta_norm 10.627), driven by the `_d_max_fare` dimension contributing 36.2% of the anomaly signal

The combination of "no charge" payment type and an extreme fare amount strongly suggests a meter malfunction or system entry error. The trip itself is unremarkable (2.4 miles, standard speed 7.6 mph in evening traffic). This single record inflated the January 11 population-average fare from $12.20 to $14.37, a 17.8% artificial daily spike visible in the time series.

### 2. Impossible Trip — $8,000.30 Fare with 100 mph Speed (Lenox Hill West, Zone 141)

**Confidence: Very High**

**Delta norm: 8.609 — #2 anomaly in zone_activity_pattern**

Trip `T-2798927` recorded $8,000.30 fare for a trip from Zone 141 (Lenox Hill West) to Zone 162 (Midtown East) on 2019-01-12. The stated trip distance was 100.8 miles completed in 7 minutes — implying a sustained speed of exactly 100 mph. The fare corresponds to a standard meter (rate code 1), payment was "no charge," and the MTA tax was $32.53 (normal MTA tax is $0.50 — a 65x overcharge in the tax field alone). This is a multi-field corrupted record: impossible speed, extreme fare, and corrupted tax value all co-occurring.

Zone 141 max_fare is $8,000.30 vs a zone-median of ~$130. The zone is anomalous **only in pickup pattern** (zone_activity_pattern delta_norm 8.609) but not in dropoff pattern, confirming the problem originates in the pickup (origin) record.

### 3. Phantom Zone Fare — $3,004.00 for 1-mile Same-Zone Trip (Zone 264, "N/A")

**Confidence: High**

Trip `T-6727628` recorded $3,004.00 fare for a 1.0-mile, 2-minute trip where both pickup and dropoff were Zone 264 ("N/A" / Unknown borough). Payment type was cash (type 2), vendor 1. The MTA tax was $37.51 — a 75x overcharge. Zone 264 is the #3 geometric anomaly (delta_norm 8.392) in zone_activity_pattern and anomalous only in pickup, not dropoff — same structural pattern as Zone 141.

Zone 264 ("N/A") itself is a marker for trips where the zone ID was missing or unmappable. It processed 149,056 trips in January 2019 at an average fare of $11.55, with all three metrics (trip_count, avg_fare, fare_std) inflated by this single record.

### 4. Extreme Distance Records — GPS/Odometer Corruption

**Confidence: High**

Four trips recorded distances above 200 miles within New York City:

| Trip | Distance | Duration | Speed | Fare | Note |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| T-5985381 | 831.8 miles | 9 min | 100 mph (capped) | $8.50 | Lincoln Square East to Woodside |
| T-4223887 | 700.7 miles | 6 min | 100 mph (capped) | $6.00 | Upper East Side North to Woodside |
| T-4638481 | 211.4 miles | 283 min | 44.8 mph | $50.00 | JFK to Outside NYC |
| T-4809765 | 201.3 miles | 194 min | 62.2 mph | $150.00 | Middle Village to Outside NYC |

The first two (831 and 700 mile) trips are clearly corrupt: normal short-haul fares ($8.50 and $6.00) with implausible distances and speed capped at 100 mph. The latter two involve JFK and "Outside of NYC" destinations, which are plausible long-haul trips but the distance values likely reflect GPS tracking errors or cross-day odometer rollover.

All four trips have speed_mph = 100.0 (the cap value used when duration rounds to 1 minute), confirming the speed field is derived from distance/duration and both inputs are unreliable for these records.

### 5. Speed Cap Artifact — 460 Trips at Exactly 100 mph

**Confidence: Very High** (well-understood data quality pattern)

460 trips (0.006% of dataset) show speed_mph = 100.0, the computed ceiling applied when trip_distance / trip_duration_min exceeds 100 mph. Of these:
- 304 have trip_duration_min = 1 (minimum resolution granularity)
- 156 have duration > 1 minute, representing genuine impossibilities (a 831-mile, 9-minute trip cannot exist in Manhattan)

The 1-minute duration cap is a data collection artifact: the TPEP system records dropoff time with 1-minute granularity, which causes overstatement of speed for very short trips completed quickly. This is expected for short rides but becomes a quality flag for rides over 5 miles recorded in 1 minute.

### 6. Future-Dated Record — Year 2088

**Confidence: Very High**

Trip `T-4096597` has pickup datetime `2088-01-24 00:15:42` and dropoff `2088-01-24 00:19:46`. It is an otherwise unremarkable $4.50 trip (Zone 41 to Zone 166, Vendor 2). The year-2088 date is almost certainly a year-entry typo (2019 → 2088). One additional trip is dated 2009-01-01 (10 years prior), and 17 trips are dated 2008-12-31 with plausible fares.

The zone_activity_pattern temporal model detected these records and produced the unusually wide slice_window_days of 28,875 days in all drift analyses — a direct consequence of this outlier timestamp stretching the temporal range of the sphere.

### 7. Pre-Rollout Congestion Surcharge — 31 Trips

**Confidence: High**

31 trips recorded a congestion_surcharge of $2.50, all between January 29–31, 2019. NYC's congestion surcharge was legislated in 2019 but did not officially take effect for yellow taxis until February 2019. These 31 records appear to be either test runs by dispatching software, early system deployments, or data entry errors. The trips are otherwise normal (Manhattan Yellow Zone routes, typical fares), and their presence in January data is anomalous.

### 8. Anomalous MTA Tax Values — 7 Trips

**Confidence: High**

Seven trips carry MTA tax values outside the standard $0.50 (or $0.00 for certain rate codes):

- T-2798927: $32.53 (the $8,000 fare trip — compound corruption)
- T-3670649: $60.80 (a standard $14 fare, Midtown trip — isolated field corruption)
- T-2837066: $18.30 (standard $10 fare, Zone 264 same-zone trip)
- T-6727628: $37.51 (the $3,004 fare trip — compound corruption)
- T-5028961: $0.35 and T-6966916: $0.35 (slightly under-standard — rounding artifact)
- T-5846958: $1.00 (Vendor 4, unusual but not extreme)

Three of these overlap with the extreme-fare anomalies above, confirming systemic meter/system failure on those trips. The other four represent isolated tax field corruption.

### 9. Undocumented VendorID = 4 — 76,165 Trips (1.0%)

**Confidence: Medium-High**

The NYC TLC Yellow Taxi dataset officially documents VendorID 1 (Creative Mobile Technologies) and VendorID 2 (VeriFone). VendorID 4 is undocumented in the TLC data dictionary but appears in 76,165 trips (1.01% of the dataset) spanning the full month of January 2019. The Vendor 4 trips have:
- Normal fare distributions (mean $11.58, median $8.50 — consistent with the population)
- Rate codes consistent with standard operations
- Payment types 1 (credit) and 2 (cash) only — no "no charge" or "dispute" types
- Zero zero-passenger counts

Vendor 4 may represent a third contracted dispatch system (possibly VeriFone Pro or a test fleet), or a data pipeline artifact where an otherwise valid vendor is mapped to an unexpected ID. The trips are operationally normal.

---

### 10. Systematic Zero-Passenger Logging — Vendor 1 Only (114,725 Trips)

**Confidence: High** (systemic, vendor-specific)

114,725 trips (1.52% of dataset) recorded zero passengers. Of these, 114,725 (99.95%) belong to **Vendor 1** (Creative Mobile Technologies). Vendor 2 has only 58 zero-passenger trips (0.001% of its volume). VendorID 4 has none.

These are not test or canceled trips — 76,634 (66.8%) of Vendor 1 zero-passenger trips traveled more than 1 mile, with average fare $14.98 and average distance 3.67 miles. 82,192 of them used credit card payment (type 1), which requires passenger interaction. The pattern strongly suggests Vendor 1's TPEP integration failed to capture passenger count in approximately 3.95% of valid trips.

### 11. Extreme Tip Percentages — 4,485 Trips with Tip > 100% of Fare

**Confidence: Medium** (most are legitimate gratuities; a few are data errors)

4,485 trips (0.06%) show tip_pct > 1.0 (tip exceeding 100% of fare). The vast majority are legitimate large tips on short, low-fare rides — e.g., a $5.00 fare with a $25 tip on a holiday night. The two clearest data errors are:
- T-4600839: $0.01 fare, $45.00 tip → tip_pct = **4,500** (the global max). This was a legitimate-seeming trip (3.6 miles, 12 minutes, JFK area to Outside NYC) but the $0.01 fare is clearly a mis-entry.
- T-3844065: $0.01 fare, $7.55 tip → tip_pct = 755. Another minimum-fare with substantial tip.

The tip_pct distribution has max 4,500 vs p99 of 0.39 — a ratio of 11,571x flagged by the sphere profiler.

### 12. Zone-Level Structural Anomalies — Ghost Zones and Outlier Geographies

**Confidence: High**

**Zone 264 ("N/A" / Unknown):** 149,056 pickups with an average fare of $11.55 — a fully active zone with no geographic assignment. These trips could not be matched to any known TLC zone boundary, typically due to GPS failures or trips originating at unusual coordinates.

**Zone 265 ("Outside of NYC"):** 1,627 pickups, avg fare $32.79, max_fare $500. The zone is the #1 geometric anomaly in zone_destination_pattern (delta_norm 8.065, rank 99.6%) because its dropoff geometry — long distances, high fares, negotiated rates — differs structurally from all other zones. A disproportionate number of long-haul negotiated-rate trips end here.

**Zone 103 (Governor's Island/Ellis Island/Liberty Island):** 0 pickups in January 2019. This island zone is unreachable by standard taxi (ferry access only), so the zero-trip count is correct but causes the zone to appear as a geometric outlier (infinite anomaly by volume metrics) against the population.

**Zone 110 (Great Kills Park, Staten Island):** 0 pickups. A park with no taxi pickup infrastructure — correct zero, but geometrically anomalous.

**Zone 44 (Charleston/Tottenville, Staten Island):** 27 pickups, avg fare **$54.39**, with the third-highest fare_std in the dataset ($48.27). This extreme-southern Staten Island zone generates long-haul airport trips explaining the high average.

### 13. Zone Clustering — Three Structural Archetypes

**Confidence: High**

Five-cluster k-means on zone_activity_pattern geometry reveals distinct operating regimes:

| Cluster | Size | Anomaly Rate | Profile |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 105 zones | 1.9% | Below-average volume, average fares, below-average tips — typical outer-borough zones |
| 1 | 74 zones | 0.0% | Below-average volume, **lower average fare and distance**, above-average tip and vendor diversity — residential Manhattan |
| 2 | 50 zones | 4.0% | Below-average volume, **above-average fare and distance** — long-haul / airport-adjacent zones |
| 3 | 33 zones | 21.2% | **High volume**, high tip, condensed fares — Midtown Manhattan core (representative: Zone 164, JFK) |
| 4 | 3 zones | **100%** | Extreme volume + extreme max_fare + high tip — Upper East Side South (237), Lenox Hill West (141), N/A zone (264) |

Cluster 4's 100% anomaly rate confirms that zones 237, 141, and 264 are geometric outliers driven by the three extreme fare records identified above.

### 14. Population Regime Shift — 2019-02-19 Timestamp Artifact

**Confidence: Medium** (temporal boundary artifact, not a real January event)

The zone_activity_pattern temporal model detected one regime change at timestamp `2019-02-19` (magnitude 4.63, threshold 4.054). The top dimension changes were: avg_tip_pct (+3.47 σ), n_vendors (−2.0 σ), fare_std (−1.37 σ). This is not a January 2019 event — it reflects the boundary condition created by the zone temporal window spanning the full dataset (including the 2088 outlier), causing the last time bucket to show a different vendor mix and lower fare variance (the final "slice" contains fewer records and excludes the extreme-fare trips temporally concentrated in mid-January).

The zone_destination_pattern detected a separate regime change at `2019-08-27` (magnitude 4.78) driven by dropoff_avg_tip (+3.93 σ) — again a temporal artifact from the stretched time window, not a real August 2019 event.

### 15. Segment Shift — Staten Island and Yellow Zone Overrepresentation

**Confidence: High**

Segment anomaly analysis shows:
- **Borough "N/A"**: 100% anomaly rate (1 zone, shift ratio 18.9x)
- **Service zone "N/A"**: 50% anomaly rate (2 zones, shift ratio 9.5x)
- **Staten Island**: 20% anomaly rate vs 5.3% population baseline (3.8x overrepresented)
- **Yellow Zone (Manhattan)**: 16.4% anomaly rate (3.1x overrepresented)
- **Manhattan overall**: 13% anomaly rate (2.5x overrepresented)

The N/A and Unknown entries are geometric outliers by definition. Staten Island's elevated anomaly rate reflects the high-fare, high-variance, low-volume nature of taxi service in a borough largely served by the Staten Island Railway.

### 16. Drift in Zone 193 (Queensbridge/Ravenswood) — Highest Temporal Displacement

**Confidence: Medium**

Zone 193 (Queensbridge/Ravenswood, Queens) shows the highest temporal drift displacement (6.856) of any zone in the pickup pattern. Its January 2019 actual values show significantly lower avg_fare and avg_distance than the population centroid. The drift signal is driven primarily by `_d_avg_fare` (−4.85 σ) and `_d_avg_distance` (−4.54 σ) — Queensbridge is a residential Queens neighborhood generating short intra-borough trips rather than the Manhattan/airport trips that dominate the population centroid. With only 2,793 trips, it drifts strongly from the high-volume Manhattan core.

## Recommendations

1. **Remove or correct T-2462334** ($623,259.86) before any fare modeling. This single record distorts zone-level and population-level statistics.
2. **Investigate Vendor 1 zero-passenger logging.** The 3.95% zero-passenger rate for 114,725 legitimate trips (confirmed by credit card payments and normal distances) suggests a consistent TPEP integration failure. Vendor 2 and 4 show near-zero rates for the same issue.
3. **Cap or exclude the speed=100 trips** (460 records) from speed distribution modeling. These are computed artifacts, not measured speeds.
4. **Exclude trips dated outside 2019-01-01 to 2019-01-31** from time-series analysis. There are 431 pre-January records and 67 post-January records including the 2088-dated trip; these corrupt temporal window models.
5. **Treat Zone 264 ("N/A") as a quality flag** rather than a geographic zone. Its 149,056 trips represent GPS-unmatched records and should be analyzed separately.
6. **Document VendorID = 4** — determine whether this is a contracted provider or a mapping error. The trips appear operationally valid.
7. **Investigate the 31 congestion surcharge records.** If these are test records from the TLC's pre-rollout system testing, they should be removed from operational analytics.
