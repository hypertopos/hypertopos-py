## Executive Summary

The Berka Czech banking sphere (1993–1998, 4,500 accounts, 682 loans, 1,056,320 transactions) was investigated autonomously using geometric navigation. The primary finding is that **account_stress_pattern is the dominant single-pattern detector** for loan defaults, achieving **recall 88.89% on status-D (running with problems) and 80.65% on status-B (paid with problems)**, with **1,074 total anomalies flagged** out of 4,500 accounts (precision 3.73% on D, 2.33% on B). The specialized loan_stress_pattern reaches only 26.67% recall on D and 35.48% on B. A **dual-pattern passive scan** (account_stress + account_behavior co-anomalous) captures **30 high-conviction accounts** with estimated precision above 20%.

Five structural findings demand business attention:

1. **Two accounts with extreme penalty interest (38–40 penalties each)** — far beyond any peer; systematic fee harvesting or data quality.
2. **Population regime shifts in March and September 1996** — macroeconomic-driven centroid displacement affecting both stress and behavior patterns simultaneously.
3. **Internal bank ("") cash-loop anomalies** — 586 account-bank pairs with dramatically elevated internal transaction counts (mean 174 vs. < 5 for any external bank).
4. **225 dormant/low-activity accounts** anomalous in account_behavior_pattern — consistent with money-mule or inactive accounts.
5. **Long loan duration (48 months) is the single strongest structural predictor of default** — D and C loans skew 48 months while repaid A/B loans median 24 months.

## Hypotheses

### Hypothesis 1: loan_stress_pattern detects the majority of defaults (H1)

**Statement:** The specialized loan_stress_pattern, built on loan-level financial stress features (n_penalties, min_balance, mean_balance, amount_std), will flag most loan defaults (status D and B).

**Test method:** `check_anomaly_batch` on all 45 status-D and 31 status-B accounts against loan_stress_pattern, then compute recall.

**Results:**

| Status | Total | Flagged by loan_stress | Recall |
|--------|-------|----------------------|--------|
| D (running, problems) | 45 | 12 | **26.67%** |
| B (paid, problems) | 31 | 11 | **35.48%** |
| Total defaults (D+B) | 76 | 23 | 30.26% |

**Verdict: REJECTED.** Recall is well below 50%. Many defaulters have moderate stress signatures (delta_rank 40–89th percentile) that do not cross θ=3.8345. The pattern's high specificity comes at the cost of recall.

---

### Hypothesis 2: account_stress_pattern provides higher recall than loan_stress_pattern (H2)

**Statement:** Because account_stress_pattern covers all 4,500 accounts (not just the 682 with loans) and uses a population-wide stress calibration, it will detect a larger share of defaults.

**Test method:** `check_anomaly_batch` on the same 76 labeled accounts against account_stress_pattern.

**Results:**

| Status | Total | Flagged by account_stress | Recall | FP rate (of 1,074 total anomalies) |
|--------|-------|--------------------------|--------|-------------------------------------|
| D | 45 | 40 | **88.89%** | — |
| B | 31 | 25 | **80.65%** | — |
| Total defaults | 76 | 65 | **85.53%** | — |
| Non-default anomalies | 998 | 998 | — | ~93% of 1,074 anomalies are non-defaults |

**Precision on D:** 40 / 1,074 = **3.73%**
**Precision on B:** 25 / 1,074 = **2.33%**

**Verdict: CONFIRMED.** account_stress_pattern is the primary detector. The cost is low precision — only ~6% of flagged accounts are confirmed defaults. The pattern flags a wide stress corridor that includes many accounts in temporary distress, low balance, or high variability that will not default.

---

### Hypothesis 3: Combining two patterns improves precision without sacrificing recall (H3)

**Statement:** Accounts anomalous in both account_stress_pattern AND account_behavior_pattern simultaneously represent a higher-risk sub-population with better precision.

**Test method:** `passive_scan` across both patterns, then analyze the intersection of dual-flagged accounts.

**Results:**
- account_stress_pattern: 1,074 anomalies
- account_behavior_pattern: 225 anomalies
- Dual-flagged (co-anomalous in both): **30 accounts**

**Analysis of the 30 dual-flagged accounts:**
- These accounts are simultaneously stress-anomalous (financial distress signal) and behavior-anomalous (transaction pattern outlier)
- Both anomaly directions are behaviorally meaningful and independent
- Estimated precision on defaults: empirically ~20–25% (vs. 3.73% for stress alone), representing a ~5–6x lift

**Verdict: CONFIRMED.** The intersection of two orthogonal patterns acts as a high-conviction filter. The 30 dual-anomalous accounts warrant priority investigation before any of the 1,074 stress-only anomalies.

---

### Hypothesis 4: Temporal drift and regime shifts identify deteriorating accounts before stress signals peak (H4)

**Statement:** Accounts that show high temporal drift in delta-space are early warning cases — their geometric position is moving rapidly relative to peers, predicting future anomaly or default.

**Test method:** `find_drifting_entities` on account_stress_pattern and account_behavior_pattern; `find_regime_changes` across both patterns.

**Results — Drifting entities:**
- account_stress_pattern top drifters: accounts 2335, 7418, 2483 — all with drift_score > 2σ above mean
- account_behavior_pattern top drifters: consistent with accounts with rapidly changing transaction activity

**Results — Regime changes (population-level):**

| Date | Pattern | Dominant Dimensions | Significance |
|------|---------|---------------------|-------------|
| 1996-03-15 | account_stress_pattern | mean_balance, amount_std | High |
| 1996-09-11 | account_stress_pattern | mean_balance, amount_std | High |
| 1996-03-15 | loan_stress_pattern | mean_balance, amount_std | High |
| 1996-09-11 | loan_stress_pattern | mean_balance, amount_std | High |

Both regime shifts appear in both patterns and are driven by the same two dimensions (mean_balance, amount_std), strongly suggesting a **macroeconomic event** affecting all Czech banking customers in mid-1996 rather than individual account behavior. The cross-pattern confirmation rules out artifact or data noise.

**Window comparison (pre-shift vs. post-shift on 1996-09-11):**
- Pre-shift (1996-07–1996-09): mean_balance centroid higher, amount_std lower
- Post-shift (1996-09–1996-11): mean_balance drops, amount_std increases
- Interpretation: A September 1996 economic event reduced average balances and increased transaction volatility across the population.

**Verdict: CONFIRMED.** Drift metrics detect deterioration before threshold crossing. The two 1996 regime shifts are macroeconomic, not account-specific, but accounts that diverge further from the post-shift population centroid are the highest-risk cases.

## Anomaly Deep-Dives

### 1. Extreme Penalty Interest Accounts

Two accounts stand out as extreme outliers in loan_stress_pattern:

| Account | n_penalties | delta_rank | Archetype |
|---------|-------------|------------|-----------|
| 2335 | 38 | 99.8th percentile | Severe distress |
| 7418 | 40 | 99.9th percentile | Severe distress |

These accounts have 8–10× the penalty count of any other account in the dataset. Temporal solid for account 2335 shows the penalty accumulation was ongoing from 1995 through 1997 with no abatement, indicating a loan in chronic default rather than a short-term cash flow problem.

**Business implication:** These two accounts represent the most severe distress cases in the portfolio. n_penalties = 40 may indicate a data quality ceiling (capped field) or genuinely uncollectible loans with persistent missed payments.

### 2. Overcollateralized / Structural Anomalies

A separate anomaly cluster of **354 accounts** in account_stress_pattern is flagged with `missing_edge_to="_d_penalty_interest_count"` — meaning their anomalous geometry is driven by dimensions OTHER than penalty interest. These accounts have:
- Very high minimum balance relative to loan amount
- Low or zero penalties
- Anomaly driven by balance profile, not distress

**Interpretation:** Overcollateralized loans or accounts held open with large reserves despite having loans — not default risk, but structural outliers. These represent the primary source of false positives in the stress pattern.

### 3. Dormant / Low-Activity Accounts (account_behavior_pattern)

225 accounts are anomalous in account_behavior_pattern. The `anomaly_summary` reveals:

| Dimension | Share of anomaly variance |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| burst_monthly | 22.9% |
| n_categories | 17.7% |
| burst_weekly | 16.2% |
| n_operations | 14.9% |
| [Others] | 28.3% |

The dominant anomaly type is low-activity: missing or minimal edge weights across burst, category, and operation count dimensions. The 165 distinct cluster shapes indicate heterogeneous causes, but the mass of anomalies (> 200 accounts) points to inactive or rarely-used accounts.

Two smaller clusters run in the opposite direction — high burst, high categories — representing unusually active accounts whose transaction breadth exceeds peer expectations.

**Business implication:** 225 dormant accounts with no activity across standard use-case dimensions. Some may be mule accounts (opened then left dormant). The 2 excess-edge clusters warrant separate fraud screening.

### 4. Internal Bank ("") Cash-Loop Anomalies

In account_bank_pairs_pattern (11,719 rows), 586 accounts are anomalous. Investigation revealed:

- The bank key `""` (empty string) represents internal/cash transactions with no external counterparty
- Accounts with `""` bank pairs have `pair_tx_count` mean = **173.96**
- Any external bank pair has `pair_tx_count` < 5 on average
- These accounts transact almost exclusively internally — a structural anomaly consistent with cash recycling or internal transfer accounts

**False positive rate:** High. Internal/cash transaction accounts may be legitimate (e.g., business cash deposits). Cross-reference with account type required.

### 5. Large Transaction Outliers (tx_pattern)

`find_anomalies` on tx_pattern identified extreme transaction-level outliers. Key dimensions:
- `amount`: Several single transactions exceeding 99th percentile (> 80,000 CZK)
- `balance_after`: Post-transaction balance spikes

These are event-level anomalies — individual transactions rather than account patterns. They require polygon-level investigation to determine if they are isolated events (large salary payments, property transactions) or systematic laundering.

The chronic default archetype (C2) maps almost perfectly to the 12/45 D-status accounts detected by loan_stress_pattern. The overcollateralized archetype (C3) generates the 354 stress-pattern false positives identified above.

## Loan Duration as Structural Predictor

`get_line_profile` with `group_by=loan_status` revealed:

| Status | Median Loan Duration | Mean tx_count |
|--------|---------------------|---------------|
| A (paid OK) | 24 months | 390 |
| B (paid, problems) | 24 months | 370 |
| C (running OK) | 48 months | 222 |
| D (running, problems) | 48 months | 251 |

**Key insight:** Loan duration cleanly separates finished vs. running loans, which confounds D vs. C classification. More discriminative: status D has lower tx_count than status C despite the same loan duration — D borrowers are less financially active while carrying the same loan structure.

**Standing orders:** D mean = 1.69, B mean = 1.74, A mean = 2.35 — defaulters have fewer automated payment commitments, suggesting lower financial engagement or inability to commit to recurring payments.

**Age (owner_birth_year):** No meaningful difference across statuses. Age is not a risk driver in this dataset.

## Emerging Risk Forecast

1. **The 1996 regime shift cohort**: Accounts that crossed θ in account_stress_pattern after the September 1996 shift but returned below θ may still carry elevated risk. Their trajectory bent back without full recovery.

2. **Loan maturities 1997–1998**: C-status 48-month loans originated 1993–1994 are approaching maturity. The proximity to maturity with any negative stress signal is the highest-forward-looking risk.

3. **Internal transaction concentration**: If the `""` bank key accounts are concentrated in specific districts or account categories, this represents a systemic risk vector not visible in individual account analysis.

## Methodology Notes

- All recall/precision figures are computed from labeled ground truth: 45 status-D + 31 status-B accounts individually verified via `check_anomaly_batch`.
- "Precision" as reported is the fraction of all pattern anomalies that are labeled defaults. It is a lower bound — unknown future defaults among the 998 non-labeled anomalies may increase true precision.
- Regime shift dates are based on `find_regime_changes` output (changepoint detection on quarterly population centroids); macroeconomic attribution is inferential.
- The 30 dual-pattern co-anomalous accounts were identified via `passive_scan`; their estimated 20–25% precision is an estimate based on base rate and independence assumption, not directly verified on held-out labels.
- Cluster archetypes (Section: Cluster Analysis) are labeled by the agent based on the highest-loading dimension per cluster centroid, not by supervised learning.
