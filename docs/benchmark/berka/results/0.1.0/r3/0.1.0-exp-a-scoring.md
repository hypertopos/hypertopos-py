### 4. Temporal awareness: 2/3

The agent ran `find_regime_changes` (4 calls) on both stress patterns, `find_drifting_entities` (2 calls), `dive_solid` (1 call), and `compare_time_windows` (1 call). Three regime changes were identified in account_stress_pattern (1996 Q1, Q3, 1997 Q1) and two in loan_stress_pattern (1996 Q1, Q3). The loan population shows 1.56x larger centroid shifts. The observation that `_d_penalty_interest_count` shows zero diff at changepoints is a genuinely insightful finding -- it means these were structural balance/income shifts, not overdraft events.

Drift analysis identifies accounts 5213 (D) and 11021 (B) as high-drift with low trajectory ratios, correctly interpreted as behavioral instability (oscillating rather than monotonic drift).

However, the report does NOT distinguish real temporal shifts from data boundary artifacts. The regime shifts are attributed to "economic conditions in the Czech Republic in 1995-1996 (banking sector difficulties, currency turbulence)" without considering whether they could be artifacts of account cohort composition (new accounts entering the population change the centroid), seasonal patterns, or data collection boundaries. The interpretation is rich but lacks the critical artifact-vs-genuine distinction required for full marks. Score 2.

## Key Gaps

1. **No data boundary artifact discussion (criterion 4):** The temporal regime shifts in 1996 are attributed to Czech macroeconomic conditions without considering alternative explanations: account cohort entry effects (new accounts opening shift the population centroid mechanically), data collection boundaries, or seasonal patterns. The penalty_interest_count zero-diff observation is a strong finding, but the report still does not explicitly test whether the balance/income shifts are real economic effects vs compositional artifacts. A full-marks analysis would acknowledge and rule out these alternatives.

2. **No formal statistical apparatus (criterion 6):** The report provides raw delta values, percentiles, and dimension contributions but lacks conformal p-values, repair sets (which dimensions would need to change to bring an entity below threshold), or standardized effect sizes (Cohen's d). The root cause reasoning is sound and goes beyond restating tool output, but the formal statistical language is absent. The two anomaly archetypes in loan_stress_pattern (Section 3, Hypothesis 2) demonstrate genuine analytical depth, but the rubric's 3/3 level explicitly requires "witness sets, conformal p-values, repair sets AND root cause hypotheses."

3. **Moderate tool redundancy (criterion 5):** 18 `get_line_profile` calls and 9 `anomaly_summary` calls suggest some schema re-fetching that could have been avoided by retaining earlier results. The 77-call total is well within the 60-120 band but above the <60 threshold for full marks. The investigation strategy is systematic and each major phase adds new information, but the per-call efficiency could be tighter.
