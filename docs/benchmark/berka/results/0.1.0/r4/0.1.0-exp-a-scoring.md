### 3. Cross-pattern reasoning: 2/3

The agent used contrast_populations to compare defaulters (B+D) vs performing (A+C), yielding Cohen d effect sizes across 5 dimensions. H5 cross-references hub accounts with anomaly status across patterns. Finding 6 notes tx_pattern anomalies as a separate AML axis distinct from credit risk. However, the report does NOT mention using composite_risk, composite_risk_batch, or passive_scan. The connected findings push this above a bare 2, but without composite_risk or passive_scan tool usage, full marks cannot be awarded. Score 2.

### 4. Temporal awareness: 2/3

The agent used find_regime_changes on both stress patterns, finding matching regime shifts at 1996-03-15 (magnitude 0.340 on account_stress, 0.530 on loan_stress) plus a secondary loan_stress shift at 1996-09-11 (magnitude 0.180). Cross-validation across two independent patterns strengthens the finding. The agent also ran find_drifting_entities on account_stress, identifying 23 high-drift accounts and 8 near-theta-crossing accounts as proactive intervention candidates.

The report attributes the 1996 regime shift to "Czech banking reform period 1995-1997" and provides a meaningful statistic: "61% of status-D defaults originated before 1996-03-15 vs 42% of performing loans." This connects temporal structure to business outcomes.

However, the report does NOT distinguish real temporal shifts from data boundary artifacts. The 1996-03-15 date is accepted at face value as an economic event without considering alternative explanations: cohort composition effects (new accounts entering shift the population centroid mechanically), data collection boundary artifacts, or seasonal patterns. For the 8 near-theta accounts, the report does not examine whether the drift is genuine behavioral change or an artifact of sparse recent data. The rubric level 3 explicitly requires "distinguished real shifts from data boundary artifacts." Score 2.

## Key Gaps

1. **No composite_risk or passive_scan usage (criterion 3):** The agent used contrast_populations for population-level comparison and connected findings across patterns narratively (hub orthogonality, AML vs credit risk axes). However, the rubric level 3 explicitly requires composite_risk or passive_scan tool usage. The agent performed cross-pattern reasoning but through individual pattern analyses rather than through the dedicated composite scoring tools. This is the most notable regression from r3, which scored 3/3 on this criterion via 11 composite_risk calls.

2. **No artifact-vs-genuine distinction on temporal shifts (criterion 4):** Same gap as r3. The 1996-03-15 regime shift is attributed to Czech banking reform without considering whether cohort composition effects (new accounts entering the population shift the centroid mechanically), data collection boundaries, or seasonal patterns could explain the signal. The cross-validation across two independent patterns strengthens the finding, but the report does not explicitly test or rule out alternative explanations.

3. **79 calls exceeds the <60 threshold (criterion 5):** The investigation is focused and systematic with no visible loops, but the rubric draws a hard line at 60 calls for score 3. The 79-call total is reasonable for the breadth of analysis (5 hypotheses, 76 exhaustive ground truth checks, clustering, regime detection, drift analysis, hub analysis), but exceeds the threshold.

4. **No formal GDS statistical apparatus (criterion 6):** Same gap as r3. Cohen d and sigma deviations are provided throughout, which is good. But conformal p-values, witness sets, and repair sets (core GDS explanatory tools) are absent. The root cause hypotheses are sound and go beyond restating tool output, but the formal measures required for level 3 are missing.
