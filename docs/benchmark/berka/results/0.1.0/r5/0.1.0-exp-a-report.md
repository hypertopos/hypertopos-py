## Hypotheses

### H1: High-penalty-interest accounts are genuine defaulters, not data artefacts
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

The top anomaly in both `account_stress_pattern` and `loan_stress_pattern` is account 2335, which carries 38 penalty interest charges, loan_status="D" (default), min_balance=-17,030, and balance_trend=-32,882/month. The conformal p-value is 0.001466 (extreme severity; ratio=3.02x theta). Account 2335 has been anomalous at delta_norm ~16.5 consistently across all 25 temporal slices (1993–1998), with no regime change — this is a structural default, not a transient data boundary artefact.

The top driving dimension `_d_penalty_interest_count` (contribution 79–87%) separates defaulters from healthy borrowers with mean penalty counts of 5.98 (D-status) and 8.26 (B-status) vs 0.03 (C-status) and 0.26 (A-status). Signal is real and extremely separating.

### H2: Geometric anomaly detection has meaningful but incomplete recall on labeled loan defaults
**Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED**

Ground truth: 45 D-status accounts (current default) + 31 B-status accounts (past default) = 76 labeled "bad" loans.

Batch recall validation on a 41-account sample:
- D-status (20 checked): 6 flagged as anomaly → 30% recall on sample
- B-status (21 checked): 9 flagged as anomaly → 43% recall on sample
- Combined recall on checked set: ~37% (15/41)

All anomalous flagged accounts have delta_rank_pct ≥ 90%, confirming high precision among detections. The hard misses are analyzed in detail below.

### H3: Dormant/low-activity accounts drive behavior pattern anomalies (not fraud)
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

The top 225 anomalies in `account_behavior_pattern` are uniformly accounts with minimal transaction activity: tx_count 9–16, n_categories=1, n_operations=2, burst_daily/weekly/monthly all at minimum (1.0). These are structurally dormant accounts — single-bank, single-category, never-bursting. The anomaly pattern is a uniform negative delta across all 8 behavioral dimensions. This is a data segmentation issue, not a fraud signal. Conformal p-value for account 182 (top behavioral anomaly): 0.000222.

### H4: Account-bank pair anomalies reflect high-volume concentration risk
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

The 586 anomalies in `account_bank_pairs_pattern` are dominated by "excess edges" in the positive direction — accounts with very high pair_sum_amount (top contributor: 50.3%), pair_tx_count (25.3%), and pair_amount_std (18.5%). The top hubs (accounts 3521, 243, 11138, etc.) have hub_score ~4.3M, far exceeding the mean (527K). These are high-volume accounts sending large amounts to the empty-bank counterparty ("" bank key), representing domestic transfers or internal movements with extreme concentration.

### H5: Population regime shifts in 1996 reflect macroeconomic context, not data artefacts
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

Three regime changes detected in `account_stress_pattern`:
- 1996-03-15: magnitude 0.340 (threshold 0.280), driven by mean_balance (+0.25) and amount_std (+0.23)
- 1996-09-11: magnitude 0.292, same dimensions
- 1997-03-10: magnitude 0.288, same dimensions

The shift pattern (rising mean_balance and amount_std from 1996 onward, confirmed by compare_time_windows: centroid_shift=2.32 between 1993–1995 and 1996–1998) is consistent with the Czech economic expansion of the mid-1990s. The absence of `_d_penalty_interest_count` change in any regime shift confirms the penalty dimension remains stable — economic growth did not reduce penalty incidence. These shifts represent genuine behavioral population drift, not data boundary artefacts.

### H6: Cluster 4 in loan_stress_pattern (100% anomaly rate) identifies anomalous-min_balance accounts that are NOT defaulters
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

Cluster 4 (10 entities, 100% anomaly rate) is driven purely by anomalously high `_d_min_balance` (delta 7.27–9.24 sigma above mean). These accounts (378, 4681, 3804, 9640, etc.) have min_balance of 6,392–15,099 CZK — unusually healthy. Their loan_status is predominantly C and A (performing). This is a false positive cluster: geometric anomaly detection flags them as "unusual" because their minimum balance is far above typical, but they represent the lowest-risk borrowers in the dataset. This matters: 10 of 68 anomalies (~15%) are false positives in the risk-assessment sense.

### 1. Extreme Defaulters — Immediate Action Required (HIGH CONFIDENCE)
Account 2335 (F, born 1946, north Moravia): delta_norm 16.69 (ratio 3.02x theta), penalty_count=38, min_balance=-17,030 CZK, balance_trend=-32,882/month, loan=541,200 CZK over 60 months. Combined risk score: chi2=25.04, p=4.9e-05 across 2 patterns. Anomalous in ALL 25 temporal slices. This account has been in structural distress throughout the entire observation period.

Account 7418 (F, born 1939, south Bohemia): delta_norm 16.35 (loan_stress: 11.05), penalty_count=40, min_balance=-12,205 CZK, loan_status="B". Second-worst in both stress patterns.

Account 3374 (F, born 1943, south Bohemia): delta_norm 16.28, penalty_count=33, min_balance=-7,448 CZK — notable because this account has NO loan (loan_amount=0) yet still shows extreme stress behavior. Possible unreported credit exposure.

### 2. High-Volume Bank Pair Concentration (MEDIUM CONFIDENCE)
Account 3786 (account_bank_pairs delta_norm 6.64) with 424 transactions totaling 5.9M CZK to the empty-bank partner, plus 586 total anomalous pairs. The empty-bank ("" key) is the dominant counterparty for all hub accounts. These represent domestic transfers. Top 10 hub accounts each handle 4.3M+ in geometric footprint — significantly above mean 527K. Monitoring for concentration risk warranted.

### 3. Dormant Account Segment — Segmentation Issue (HIGH CONFIDENCE)
225 accounts in `account_behavior_pattern` are anomalous due to minimal activity (1 category, 2 operations, burst=1). These are legitimate low-activity customers who appear anomalous against the general population but pose no risk. The alias system correctly isolates this: `low_engagement_accounts` captures 3,802 (84.5%) of the population as a separate segment. No action required, but the anomaly budget is partially consumed by this structural segment.

### 4. Emerging Pre-Default Accounts (MEDIUM CONFIDENCE)

### 5. Transaction Pattern Anomalies — Missing Operations/Bank Data (MEDIUM CONFIDENCE)

This is structural: cash operations do not require a counterparty bank. Not a data quality issue, but a natural segment of the transaction population.

Loan defaults not caught by geometric anomaly detection in the checked sample:

| Account | Status | penalty_count | min_balance | delta_rank_pct | Notes |
|---------|--------|--------------|-------------|---------------|-------|
| 426 | D | 2 | -821 | 50.9% | Low penalty, shallow overdraft |
| 442 | D | 1 | -543 | 60.4% | Very low penalty count |
| 472 | D | 2 | -2,756 | 73.6% | Moderate balance; high amount_std |
| 37 | D | 1 | -1,011 | 52.1% | Classic shallow default |
| 2051 | D | 3 | -2,805 | 44.7% | Below median; not anomalous |
| 3967 | D | 0 | -2,920 | 81.2% | Zero penalties — possible recent default |
| 1247 | D | 1 | -975 | 66.1% | Low-risk profile despite D status |

**Pattern of hard misses:** Accounts with D status but penalty_interest_count ≤ 3 and min_balance in the -500 to -3,000 range are systematically missed. The loan_stress_pattern theta was calibrated against the full population; defaults with mild stress indicators fall in the 50–82nd percentile — elevated but below theta. The detector has high precision (only catches genuinely distressed cases) at the cost of recall on "soft" defaults.

**Why it matters:** Approximately 60–70% of D-status accounts are missed by the current theta. These are accounts that defaulted through income shortfall or structural inability to pay rather than through visible financial stress signatures. An alternative detection approach using `loan_status` as a supervised label, or lowering theta using radius=1.5–2.0, would increase recall.

B-status hard misses include accounts 5066 (delta_rank_pct 25.7%), 5428 (56.5%), 790 (44.4%), 1993 (59.4%) — all with zero or low penalty counts and modest overdrafts, again fitting the "quiet default" pattern.

## Temporal Analysis

### Regime Changes (account_stress_pattern)
Three detected regime changes between 1996–1997 (magnitudes 0.288–0.340). All driven by rising mean_balance and amount_std. Interpretation: Czech economic expansion mid-1990s lifted the population mean, temporarily shifting the baseline. The `_d_penalty_interest_count` dimension showed zero change across all regime shifts, confirming that penalty incidence is structurally stable and independent of macro conditions.

**Artifact assessment:** Not artifacts. The centroid_shift=2.32 across the 1993–1995 vs 1996–1998 windows is large but consistent with documented economic history. The theta calibrated against the full period appropriately accounts for this.

### Individual Drift (loan_stress_pattern)
Top drifting entities (displacement ~6.96 across all top-10) are dominated by high-balance, high-amount_std accounts with loan_status A or C. These are accounts that grew substantially over the 1993–1998 period — legitimate wealth accumulation, not distress. displacement_current for account 4616 is 6.37 (amount_std and mean_balance driving), reputation=0.89 (high anomaly persistence).

Notable: account 5213 (loan_status D) appears in top-10 drifters with path_length=23.81, ratio=0.26 (oscillating). This suggests unstable behavior before default — a pre-default behavioral signature.

### Solid History (account 2335)
Delta_norm stable at ~16.5 across all 25 slices (1993–1998). This entity was anomalous from first observation. The 1996 population shift is visible in slices 13–14 (delta_snapshot changes from [-1.45, -2.43, ...] to [+0.07, -0.80, ...] on dimensions 0 and 1), but dimension 2 (penalty_interest_count at +15.13) never changes. The distress is static and overwhelmingly penalty-driven throughout the entire period.

## Data Quality Notes

1. **FTS index limitation:** FTS queries for short single-character values ("D", "B") return at most 20 results regardless of offset — the index appears to score and cap results rather than enumerate. Full ground truth validation of all 45 D-status and 31 B-status accounts was not achievable via FTS; a dedicated filter API would be needed.

2. **No data quality issues detected:** `detect_data_quality_issues(loan_stress_pattern)` returned no findings. Edge coverage is adequate.

3. **Inactive pairs (54.56%):** `account_bank_pairs_pattern` has inactive_ratio=0.5456, meaning over half of the 11,719 entities have no alive edges. This is expected — many (account, bank) pairs are historical and no longer active.

4. **Account 3374 anomaly without loan:** This account appears in `account_stress_pattern` with delta_norm 16.28 and penalty_count=33 but has has_loan=absent and loan_amount=0. It appears in the general account population but not in loan_accounts. Either the loan expired before the snapshot, or the account carries overdraft obligations. Warrants investigation.

5. **Missing balance_trend/min_balance edges:** Multiple anomaly clusters in stress patterns are driven by missing `_d_min_balance`, `_d_balance_trend`, and `_d_mean_balance` edges. This causes structural anomalies from missing data rather than genuine distress — approximately 100–150 of the 493 stress anomalies may be missing-data artefacts.

## Recommendations

1. **Immediate review queue:** Accounts 2335, 7418, 3374, 3678, 5927, 10656 — all with delta_norm > 12 in account_stress_pattern and/or loan_stress_pattern. Focus on those with negative balance_trend and high penalty counts.

2. **Widen detection radius for regulatory compliance:** Use `radius=1.5` on loan_stress_pattern to catch the "quiet default" segment (D-status with low penalty counts). Expected recall improvement: from ~37% to ~60–65% based on delta_rank_pct distribution (accounts at 60–89th percentile would enter at 1.5x theta).

3. **Separate dormant-account anomalies:** The `low_engagement_accounts` alias (3,802 accounts) should be excluded from the standard anomaly queue — their anomaly signal is behavioral segmentation, not risk. Create a separate alert tier for this group.

4. **Monitor emerging accounts:** Accounts 1589, 7465 are on trajectories toward crossing theta in account_behavior_pattern within 1–2 temporal windows. Pre-emptive outreach recommended.

5. **Investigate account 3374:** Extreme stress signal (penalty_count=33, min_balance=-7,448) without a recorded loan suggests untracked credit exposure or expired loan with ongoing penalties.

6. **Hub concentration monitoring:** Top-10 hubs (accounts 3521, 243, 11138, 30, 2838) each manage 4.32M+ in hub score against the empty-bank partner. While likely legitimate, the concentration warrants AML review for unusually large domestic transfer patterns.

7. **Fix missing-dimension artefacts:** Approximately 15–20% of anomalies in stress patterns are driven by missing `_d_min_balance`, `_d_balance_trend` edges rather than genuine distress. Improve ETL completeness or use imputation to reduce false positive rate.
