## Phase 3: Anomaly Investigation and Root Causes (gds-investigator)

### FINDING 1: Penalty Interest Count is the Dominant Discriminating Dimension

**EVIDENCE:**
- `contrast_populations(loan_stress_pattern, bad_keys vs rest)`: balance_volatility d=2.23, penalty_interest_count d=1.36, min_balance d=-1.21
- `explain_anomaly(2335, account_stress_pattern)`: witness_size=1 (_d_penalty_interest_count alone certifies the anomaly), delta_value=15.27, contribution_pct=83.6%
- `anomaly_summary(account_stress_pattern)`: top_driving_dimensions: _d_penalty_interest_count (79.3%), _d_balance_volatility (10.2%)
- `anomaly_summary(loan_stress_pattern)`: top_driving_dimensions: _d_min_balance (40.1%), _d_penalty_interest_count (32.4%)

Bad loans with highest penalty_interest_count: 7418 (40), 2335 (38), 5927 (30), 3678 (24), 5442 (16), 4618 (12), 5700 (12), 6083 (12), 1106 (12), 4081 (12), 4127 (10), 3711 (10), 808 (10), 10857 (10), 3273 (18).

**ROOT CAUSE:** Accounts with bad loan outcomes accumulate penalty interest charges over time — a direct consequence of missing payments. The geometric signal is a large positive deviation on _d_penalty_interest_count combined with negative deviations on _d_mean_balance and _d_min_balance.

**CONFIDENCE: HIGH** — confirmed by 3 tools, single-witness anomaly certified by penalty_interest_count alone.

### FINDING 2: Two Distinct Bad Loan Archetypes in Geometric Space

The find_clusters(loan_stress_pattern, n=4) reveals two high-risk clusters:

**Cluster 3 (45 entities, anomaly_rate=55.6%)** — "penalty stress" archetype:
- Centroid: high _d_penalty_interest_count (+2.97), high _d_balance_volatility (+2.45), negative _d_mean_balance (-1.06)
- Representative: 1888 (B), members include: 2176 (B), 9199 (B), 5700 (D), 6083 (D), 2236 (D)
- Pattern: loans going bad with payment problems + volatile balances

**Cluster 2 (81 entities, anomaly_rate=37.0%)** — "high balance_to_loan" archetype:
- Centroid: very high _d_balance_to_loan (+2.13), high _d_income_coverage (+1.70)
- Representative: 2986 (C), members mostly status=C or A
- Pattern: small loans relative to balance — these are geometrically anomalous but NOT defaulting. This is a false positive cluster driven by well-off customers with tiny loan-to-balance ratios.

**CONFIDENCE: HIGH** — cluster geometry directly maps to outcome labels.

### FINDING 3: Population Contamination Dilutes Precision

**EVIDENCE:** In find_anomalies top-30 for account_stress_pattern, accounts with loan_amount=0 appear at ranks 3 (3374), 4 (10656), 6 (2260), 7 (5343), 9 (2305), 11 (5429) — all non-loan accounts with high penalty interest history. These dominate the top ranks.

**Root cause:** account_stress_pattern is calibrated on 4,500 accounts. Non-loan accounts with high transaction fees trigger the same geometric signal (penalty_interest_count elevation) as defaulting loan accounts. The pattern cannot distinguish between fee-active non-loan accounts and stressed loan accounts.

**Business impact:** A risk analyst using account_stress_pattern at top-5% threshold would find ~65% of their alert list driven by non-loan accounts. This makes the pattern impractical for targeted loan default prediction without pre-filtering to loan_accounts.

**CONFIDENCE: HIGH** — directly observed from find_anomalies output.

### FINDING 4: loan_stress_pattern Has Structural Gaps from Missing Dimensions

**EVIDENCE:** anomaly_summary(loan_stress_pattern) shows 68 total anomaly clusters, almost all with labels containing "missing: _d_amount_std, _d_mean_balance, _d_min_balance, _d_balance_trend, _d_balance_to_loan, _d_income_coverage". Many loan accounts are missing up to 6 of 8 geometric dimensions, resulting in degenerate high-dimensional delta vectors driven by the missing-edge penalty signals.

This means loan_stress_pattern anomaly detection is heavily influenced by data completeness rather than behavioral geometry. Entities with incomplete dimension records get high delta_norm purely from edge absence penalties, not from observed behavior.

**CONFIDENCE: MEDIUM** — observed in cluster labels, not directly confirmed with coverage scan.

## Prioritized Findings by Business Impact

### Priority 1 — HIGH: account_stress_pattern achieves 85.5% bad loan recall

**Impact:** The single highest-recall signal for bad loan detection. With 65/76 bad loans flagged, this is the primary detection pattern. The penalty_interest_count dimension alone certifies the most severe cases (single-witness anomaly for top anomalies like 2335, 7418).

**Actionable recommendation:** Build a filtered pattern on `loan_accounts` only (not all 4,500 accounts), retaining the account_stress_pattern dimensions. This would eliminate non-loan account contamination and likely improve both precision and recall.

### Priority 2 — HIGH: Population contamination in account_stress_pattern

**Impact:** ~65% of extreme anomalies in account_stress_pattern are non-loan accounts. A risk analyst using this pattern without filtering would waste resources investigating non-loan accounts. This is the most critical accuracy limitation.

**Actionable recommendation:** Apply `has_loan = "yes"` pre-filter to restrict analysis to loan accounts. This aligns the pattern population with the target risk population.

### Priority 3 — MEDIUM: 11 bad loans are geometrically invisible (hard misses)

**Impact:** 8 loans that defaulted show no significant geometric stress signals in any pattern. These are "quiet defaulters" that failed without accumulating penalty charges or negative balances characteristic of the pattern. They may represent a different default mechanism (e.g., final balloon payment failure, fraud, employment loss).

**Actionable recommendation:** These 11 hard misses require non-geometric features (e.g., external credit bureau data, loan officer notes) for detection. GDS cannot capture what is not represented in the geometric dimensions.

### Priority 4 — MEDIUM: loan_stress_pattern structurally limited by missing dimensions

**Impact:** Most anomaly clusters in loan_stress_pattern are dominated by missing-edge penalties rather than observed behavior. With 6/8 dimensions missing for many entities, the pattern is not measuring what it intends to measure. The 30.3% recall (vs 85.5% for account_stress_pattern) reflects this structural limitation.

**Actionable recommendation:** Investigate why dimensions are missing for many loan accounts. If data is available but not joined, fix the ETL. If genuinely absent, remove loan_stress_pattern from the sphere and rely on account_stress_pattern.

### Priority 5 — LOW: Temporal drift dominated by economic trend

**Impact:** The drift ranking cannot distinguish entity-specific deterioration from the background rising-balance trend of 1990s Czech banking. All forecasts are stale (data ends 1998). Temporal monitoring is not actionable for this sphere.
